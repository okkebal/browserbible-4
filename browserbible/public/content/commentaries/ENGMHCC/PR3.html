1 Verses 26-27 Those who fear the Lord so as to obey and serve him, have a strong ground of confidence, and will be preserved. Let us seek to this Fountain of life, that we may escape the snares of death.
2 Verse 28 Let all that wish well to the kingdom of Christ, do what they can, that many may be added to his church.
3 Verse 29 A mild, patient man is one that learns of Christ, who is Wisdom itself. Unbridled passion is folly made known.
4 Verse 30 An upright, contented, and benevolent mind, tends to health.
5 Verse 31 To oppress the poor is to reproach our Creator.
6 Verse 32 The wicked man has his soul forced from him; he dies in his sins, under the guilt and power of them. But godly men, though they have pain and some dread of death, have the blessed hope, which God, who cannot lie, has given them.
7 Verse 33 Wisdom possesses the heart, and thus regulates the affections and tempers.
8 Verse 34 Piety and holiness always promote industry, sobriety, and honesty.
9 Verse 35 The great King who reigns over heaven and earth, will reward faithful servants who honour his gospel by the proper discharge of the duties of their stations: he despises not the services of the lowest.
11 Chapter 15 Verse 1 A right cause will be better pleaded with meekness than with passion. Nothing stirs up anger like grievous words.
12 Verse 2 He that has knowledge, is to use it aright, for the good of others.
13 Verse 3 Secret sins, services, and sorrows, are under God's eye. This speaks comfort to saints, and terror to sinners.
14 Verse 4 A good tongue is healing to wounded consciences, by comforting them; to sin-sick souls, by convincing them; and it reconciles parties at variance.
15 Verse 5 If instruction is despised, reprove men rather than suffer them to go on undisturbed in the way to ruin.
16 Verse 6 The wealth of worldly men increases their fears and suspicions, adds strength to their passions, and renders the fear of death more distressing.
17 Verse 7 We use knowledge aright when we disperse it; but the heart of the foolish has nothing to disperse that is good.
18 Verses 8-9 The wicked put other things in the stead of Christ's atonement, or in the place of holy obedience. Praying graces are his gift, and the work of his Spirit, with which he is well pleased.

20 Verse 10 He that hates reproof shall perish in his sins, since he would not be parted from them.
21 Verse 11 There is nothing that can be hid from the eyes of God, not even man's thoughts.
22 Verse 12 A scorner cannot bear to reflect seriously within his own heart.
23 Verse 13 A gloomy, impatient, unthankful spirit, springing from pride and undue attachment to worldly objects, renders a man uneasy to himself and others.
24 Verse 14 A wise man seeks to gain more wisdom, growing in grace and in the knowledge of Christ. But a carnal mind rests contented, flattering itself.
25 Verse 15 Some are much in affliction, and of a sorrowful spirit. Such are to be pitied, prayed for, and comforted. And others serve God with gladness of heart, and it prompts their obedience, yet they should rejoice with trembling.
26 Verses 16-17 Believers often have enough when worldly eyes see little; the Lord is with them, without the cares, troubles, and temptations which are with the wealth of the wicked.

28 Verse 18 He that is slow to anger, not only prevents strife, but appeases it, if kindled.
29 Verse 19 Those who have no heart to their work, pretend that they cannot do their work without hardship and danger. And thus many live always in doubt about their state, because always in neglect of some duty.
30 Verse 20 Those who treat an aged mother or a father with contempt or neglect, show their own folly.
31 Verse 21 Such as are truly wise, study that their thoughts, words, and actions should be regular, sincere, and holy.
32 Verse 22 If men will not take time and pains to deliberate, they are not likely to bring any thing to pass.
33 Verse 23 Wisdom is needed to suit our discourse to the occasions.
34 Verse 24 A good man sets his affections on things above; his way leads directly thither.
35 Verse 25 Pride is the ruin of multitudes. But those who are in affliction God will support.
