1 Verse 21 The man whose wisdom dwells in his heart, will be found more truly prudent than many who possess shining talents.
2 Verse 22 As waters to a thirsty land, so is a wise man to his friends and neighbours.
3 Verse 23 The wise man's self-knowledge, always suggests something proper to be spoken to others.
4 Verse 24 The word of God cures the diseases that weaken our souls.
5 Verse 25 This is caution to all, to take heed of deceiving themselves as to their souls.
6 Verse 26 We must labour for the meat which endureth to everlasting life, or we must perish.

11 Verse 31 Old people especially should be found in the way of religion and godliness.
12 Verse 32 To overcome our own passions, requires more steady management, than obtaining victory over an enemy.
13 Verse 33 All the disposal of Providence concerning our affairs, we must look upon to be the determining what we referred to God; and we must be reconciled to them accordingly. Blessed are those that give themselves up to the will of God; for he knows what is good for them.
15 Chapter 17 Verse 1 These words recommend family love and peace, as needful for the comfort of human life.
16 Verse 2 The wise servant is more deserving, and more likely to appear one of the family, than a profligate son.
17 Verse 3 God tries the heart by affliction. He thus has often shown the sin remaining in the heart of the believer.
18 Verse 4 Flatterers, especially false teachers, are welcome to those that live in sin.
19 Verse 5 Those that laugh at poverty, treat God's providence and precepts with contempt.
20 Verse 6 It is an honour to children to have wise and godly parents continued to them, even after they are grown up and settled in the world.
21 Verse 7 A fool, in Solomon's Proverbs, signifies a wicked man, whom excellent speech does not become, because his conversation contradicts it.
22 Verse 8 Those who set their hearts upon money, will do any thing for it. What influence should the gifts of God have on our hearts!
23 Verse 9 The way to preserve peace is to make the best of every thing; not to notice what has been said or done against ourselves.
