1 Verse 27 The covetous man lets none of his family have rest or enjoyment. And greediness of gain often tempts to projects that bring ruin.
2 Verse 28 A good man is proved to be a wise man by this; he governs his tongue well.
3 Verse 29 God sets himself at a distance from those who set him at defiance.
4 Verse 30 How delightful to the humbled soul to hear the good report of salvation by the Lord Jesus Christ!
5 Verse 31 Faithful, friendly reproofs help spiritual life, and lead to eternal life.
6 Verse 32 Sinners undervalue their own souls; therefore they prefer the body before the soul, and wrong the soul to please the body.
7 Verse 33 The fear of the Lord will dispose us to search the Scriptures with reverence; and it will cause us to follow the leadings of the Holy Spirit. While we humbly place all our dependence on the grace of God, we are exalted in the righteousness of Christ.
9 Chapter 16 Verse 1 The renewing grace of God alone prepares the heart for every good work. This teaches us that we are not sufficient of ourselves to think or speak any thing wise and good.
10 Verse 2 Ignorance, pride, and self-flattery render us partial judges respecting our own conduct.
11 Verse 3 Roll the burden of thy care upon God, and leave it with him, by faith and dependence on him.
12 Verse 4 God makes use of the wicked to execute righteous vengeance on each other; and he will be glorified by their destruction at last.
13 Verse 5 Though sinners strengthen themselves and one another, they shall not escape God's judgments.
14 Verse 6 By the mercy and truth of God in Christ Jesus, the sins of believers are taken away, and the power of sin is broken.
15 Verse 7 He that has all hearts in his hand, can make a man's enemies to be at peace with him.
16 Verse 8 A small estate, honestly come by, will turn to better account than a great estate ill-gotten.
17 Verse 9 If men make God's glory their end, and his will their rule, he will direct their steps by his Spirit and grace. 10. Let kings and judges of the earth be just, and rule in the fear of God.
19 Verse 11 To observe justice in dealings between man and man is God's appointment.
20 Verse 12 The ruler that uses his power aright, will find that to be his best security.
21 Verse 13 Put those in power who know how to speak to the purpose.
22 Verses 14-15 Those are fools, who, to obtain the favour of an earthly prince, throw themselves out of God's favour.
23 Verses 14-15 Those are fools, who, to obtain the favour of an earthly prince, throw themselves out of God's favour.
24 Verse 16 There is joy and satisfaction of spirit, only in getting wisdom.
25 Verse 17 A sincerely religious man keeps at a distance from every appearance of evil. Happy is the man that walks in Christ, and is led by the Spirit of Christ.
26 Verse 18 When men defy God's judgments, and think themselves far from them, it is a sign they are at the door. Let us not fear the pride of others, but fear pride in ourselves.
27 Verse 19 Humility, though it exposes to contempt in the world, is much better than high-spiritedness, which makes God an enemy. He that understands God's word shall find good.
