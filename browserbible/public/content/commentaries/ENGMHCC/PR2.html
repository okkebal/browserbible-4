1 Verse 4 There can be no advantage without something which, though of little moment, will affright the indolent.
2 Verse 5 A conscientious witness will not dare to represent anything otherwise than according to his knowledge.
3 Verse 6 A scorner treats Divine things with contempt. He that feels his ignorance and unworthiness will search the Scriptures in a humble spirit.
4 Verse 7 We discover a wicked man if there is no savour of piety in his discourse.
5 Verse 8 We are travellers, whose concern is, not to spy out wonders, but to get to their journey's end; to understand the rules we are to walk by, also the ends we are to walk toward. The bad man cheats himself, and goes on in his mistake.
6 Verse 9 Foolish and profane men consider sin a mere trifle, to be made light of rather than mourned over. Fools mock at the sin-offering; but those that make light of sin, make light of Christ.
7 Verse 10 We do not know what stings of conscience, or consuming passions, torment the prosperous sinner. Nor does the world know the peace of mind a serious Christian enjoys, even in poverty and sickness.
8 Verse 11 Sin ruins many great families; whilst righteousness often raises and strengthens even mean families.
9 Verse 12 The ways of carelessness, of worldliness, and of sensuality, seem right to those that walk in them; but self-deceivers prove self-destroyers. See the vanity of carnal mirth.
11 Verse 14 Of all sinners backsliders will have the most terror when they reflect on their own ways.
12 Verse 15 Eager readiness to believe what others say, has ever proved mischievous. The whole world was thus ruined at first. The man who is spiritually wise, depends on the Saviour alone for acceptance. He is watchful against the enemies of his salvation, by taking heed to God's word.
13 Verse 16 Holy fear guards against every thing unholy.
14 Verse 17 An angry man is to be pitied as well as blamed; but the revengeful is more hateful.
15 Verse 18 Sin is the shame of sinners; but wisdom is the honour of the wise.
16 Verse 19 Even bad men acknowledge the excellency of God's people.
17 Verse 20 Friendship in the world is governed by self-interest. It is good to have God our Friend; he will not desert us.
18 Verse 21 To despise a man for his employment or appearance is a sin.
19 Verse 22 How wisely those consult their own interest, who not only do good, but devise it!
20 Verse 23 Labour of the head, or of the hand, will turn to some good account. But if men's religion runs all out in talk and noise, they will come to nothing.
21 Verse 24 The riches of men of wisdom and piety enlarge their usefulness.
22 Verse 25 An upright man will venture the displeasure of the greatest, to bring truth to light.
