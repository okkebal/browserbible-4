1 Verse 11 Satan, and the messengers of Satan, shall be let loose upon an evil man.
2 Verse 12 Let us watch over our own passions, and avoid the company of furious men.
3 Verse 13 To render evil for good is devilish. He that does so, brings a curse upon his family.
4 Verse 14 What danger there is in the beginning of strife! Resist its earliest display; and leave it off, if it were possible, before you begin.
5 Verse 15 It is an offence to God to acquit the guilty, or to condemn those who are not guilty.
6 Verse 16 Man's neglect of God's favour and his own interest is very absurd.
7 Verse 17 No change of outward circumstances should abate our affection for our friends or relatives. But no friend, except Christ, deserves unlimited confidence. In Him this text did receive, and still receives its most glorious fulfilment.
8 Verse 18 Let not any wrong their families. Yet Christ's becoming Surety for men, was a glorious display of Divine wisdom; for he was able to discharge the bond.
9 Verse 19 If we would keep a clear conscience and a quiet mind, we must shun all excitements to anger. And a man who affects a style of living above his means, goes the way to ruin.
10 Verse 20 There is nothing got by ill designs. And many have paid dear for an unbridled tongue.
11 Verse 21 This speaks very plainly what many wise and good men feel very strongly, how grievous it is to have a foolish, wicked child.
12 Verse 22 It is great mercy that God gives us leave to be cheerful, and cause to be cheerful, if by his grace he gives us hearts to be cheerful.
13 Verse 23 The wicked are ready to part with their money, though loved, that they may not suffer for their crimes.
14 Verse 24 The prudent man keeps the word of God continually in view. But the foolish man cannot fix his thoughts, nor pursue any purpose with steadiness.
15 Verse 25 Wicked children despise the authority of their father, and the tenderness of their mother.
16 Verse 26 It is very wrong to find fault for doing what is duty.
17 Verses 27-28 A man may show himself to be a wise man, by the good temper of his mind, and by the good government of his tongue. He is careful when he does speak, to speak to the purpose. God knows his heart, and the folly that is bound there; therefore he cannot be deceived in his judgment as men may be.

20 Chapter 18 Verse 1 If we would get knowledge and grace, we must try all methods of improving ourselves.
21 Verse 2 Those make nothing to purpose, of learning or religion, whose only design is to have something to make a show with.
22 Verse 3 As soon as sin entered, shame followed.
23 Verse 4 The well-spring of wisdom in the heart of a believer, continually supplies words of wisdom.
24 Verse 5 The merits of a cause must be looked to, not the person.
25 Verses 6-7 What mischief bad men do to themselves by their ungoverned tongues!
26 Verses 6-7 What mischief bad men do to themselves by their ungoverned tongues!
27 Verse 8 How base are those that sow contention! and what fatal effects may be expected from small beginnings of jealousy!
28 Verse 9 Omissions of duty, and in duty, are fatal to the soul, as well as commissions of sin.
29 Verses 10-11 The Divine power, made known in and through our Lord Jesus Christ, forms a strong tower for the believer, who relies on the Lord. How deceitful the defence of the rich man, who has his portion and treasure in this world! It is a strong city and a high wall only in his own conceit; for it will fail when most in need. They will be exposed to the just wrath of that Judge whom they despised as a Saviour.

31 Verse 12 After the heart has been lifted up with pride, a fall comes. But honour shall be the reward of humility.
32 Verse 13 Eagerness, with self-conceit, will expose to shame.
33 Verse 14 Firmness of mind supports under many pains and trials. But when the conscience is tortured with remorse, no human fortitude can bear the misery; what then will hell be?
34 Verse 15 We must get knowledge, not only into our heads, but into our hearts.
35 Verse 16 Blessed be the Lord, who makes us welcome to come to his throne, without money and without price. May his gifts make room for him in our souls.
