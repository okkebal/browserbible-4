1 Verses 2-3 Though the righteous may be poor, the Lord will not suffer him to want what is needful for spiritual life.
2 Verse 4 Those who are fervent in spirit, serving the Lord, are likely to be rich in faith, and rich in good works.
3 Verse 5 Here is just blame of those who trifle away opportunities, both for here and for hereafter.
4 Verse 6 Abundance of blessings shall abide on good men; real blessings.
5 Verse 7 Both the just and the wicked must die; but between their souls there is a vast difference.
6 Verse 8 The wise in heart puts his knowledge in practice.
7 Verse 9 Dissemblers, after all their shuffling, will be exposed.
8 Verse 10 Trick and artifice will be no excuse for iniquity.
9 Verse 11 The good man's mouth is always open to teach, comfort, and correct others.
10 Verse 12 Where there is hatred, every thing stirs up strife. By bearing with each other, peace and harmony are preserved.
11 Verse 13 Those that foolishly go on in wicked ways, prepare rods for themselves.
12 Verse 14 Whatever knowledge may be useful, we must lay it up, that it may not be to seek when we want it. The wise gain this wisdom by reading, by hearing the word, by meditation, by prayer, by faith in Christ, who is made of God unto us wisdom.
13 Verse 15 This refers to the common mistakes both of rich and poor, as to their outward condition. Rich people's wealth exposes them to many dangers; while a poor man may live comfortably, if he is content, keeps a good conscience, and lives by faith.
14 Verse 16 Perhaps a righteous man has no more than what he works hard for, but that labour tends to life.
15 Verse 17 The traveller that has missed his way, and cannot bear to be told of it, and to be shown the right way, must err still.
16 Verse 18 He is especially a fool who thinks to hide anything from God; and malice is no better.
17 Verse 19 Those that speak much, speak much amiss. He that checks himself is a wise man, and therein consults his own peace.
18 Verses 20-21 The tongue of the just is sincere, freed from the dross of guile and evil design. Pious discourse is spiritual food to the needy. Fools die for want of a heart, so the word is; for want of thought.

20 Verse 22 That wealth which is truly desirable, has no vexation of spirit in the enjoyment; no grief for the loss; no guilt by the abuse of it. What comes from the love of God, has the grace of God for its companion.
21 Verse 23 Only foolish and wicked men divert themselves with doing harm to others, or tempting to sin.
