1 Verse 28 Mercy and truth are the glories of God's throne.
2 Verse 29 Both young and old have their advantages; and let neither despise or envy the other.
3 Verse 30 Severe rebukes sometimes do a great deal of good. But such is the corruption of nature, that men are loth to be rebuked for their sins. If God uses severe afflictions, to purify our hearts and fit us for his service, we have cause to be very thankful.
5 Chapter 21 Verse 1 The believer, perceiving that the Lord rules every heart as he sees fit, like the husbandman who turns the water through his grounds as he pleases, seeks to have his own heart, and the hearts of others, directed in his faith, fear, and love.
6 Verse 2 We are partial in judging ourselves and our actions.
7 Verse 3 Many deceive themselves with a conceit that outward devotions will excuse unrighteousness.
8 Verse 4 Sin is the pride, the ambition, the glory, the joy, and the business of wicked men.
9 Verse 5 The really diligent employ foresight as well as labour.
10 Verse 6 While men seek wealth by unlawful practices, they seek death.
11 Verse 7 Injustice will return upon the sinner, and will destroy him here and for ever.
12 Verse 8 The way of mankind by nature is froward and strange.
13 Verse 9 It is best to shun bitter contention by pouring out the heart before God. For by prudence and patience, with constant prayer, the cross may be removed.
14 Verse 10 The evil desires of a wicked man's heart, lead to baseness in his conduct.
15 Verse 11 The simple may be made wise by punishments on the wicked, and by instructions to those who are willing to be taught.
16 Verse 12 Good men envy not the prosperity of evil-doers; they see there is a curse on them.
17 Verse 13 Such as oppress the poor by beating down wages, such as will not relieve according to their ability those in distress, and those in authority who neglect to do justice, stop their ears at the cry of the poor. But doubtless care is to be used in the exercise of charity.
18 Verse 14 If money can conquer the fury of the passions, shall reason, the fear of God, and the command of Christ, be too weak to bridle them?
