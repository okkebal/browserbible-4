1 O God, the proud are risen against me, and the assemblies of violent [men] have {k} sought after my soul; and have not set thee before them. (k) He shows that there can be no moderation or equity where proud tyrants reign, and that the lack of God's fear is as a privilege to all vice and cruelty.
3 O turn unto me, and have mercy upon me; give thy strength unto thy servant, and save the {l} son of thine handmaid. (l) He boasts not of his own virtues, but confesses that God of his free goodness has always been merciful to him, and given him power against his enemies, as to one of his own household.
6 His {a} foundation [is] in the holy mountains. (a) God chose that place among the hills to establish Jerusalem and his temple.
