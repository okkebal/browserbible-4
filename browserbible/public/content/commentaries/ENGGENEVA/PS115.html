1 There will I make the {l} horn of David to bud: I have ordained a lamp for mine anointed. (l) Though his force for a time seemed to be broken, yet he promises to restore it.
4 Behold, how good and how pleasant [it is] for brethren to dwell {a} together in unity! (a) Because the greatest part was against David, though some favoured him, yet when he was established king at length, they joined all together like brothers: and therefore he shows by these similarities the convenience of brotherly love.
5 [It is] like the precious {b} ointment upon the head, that ran down upon the beard, [even] Aaron's beard: that went down to the skirts of his garments; (b) The ointment was a figure of the graces which come from Christ the head of his Church.
6 As the dew of {c} Hermon, [and as the dew] that descended upon the mountains of Zion: for {d} there the LORD commanded the blessing, [even] life for evermore. (c) By Hermon and Zion he means the plentiful country about Jerusalem. (d) Where there is such concord.
8 Behold, bless ye the LORD, all [ye] {a} servants of the LORD, which by night stand in the house of the LORD. (a) You who are Levites and chiefly appointed to this office.
9 Lift up your {b} hands [in] the sanctuary, and bless the LORD. (b) For their charge was not only to keep the temple, but to pray there and to give God thanks.
10 The LORD that {c} made heaven and earth bless thee out of Zion. (c) And therefore has all power, bless you with his fatherly love declared in Zion. Thus the Levites used to praise the Lord, and bless the people.
12 The LORD that {c} made heaven and earth bless thee out of Zion. (c) And therefore has all power, bless you with his fatherly love declared in Zion. Thus the Levites used to praise the Lord, and bless the people.
13 Ye that {a} stand in the house of the LORD, in the {b} courts of the house of our God, (a) You Levites who are in his sanctuary. (b) Meaning, the people: for the people and Levites had their courts, which were separate places of the temple.
15 For the LORD hath {c} chosen Jacob unto himself, [and] Israel for his peculiar treasure. (c) That is, has freely loved the posterity of Abraham.
17 Whatsoever the LORD pleased, [that] {d} did he in heaven, and in earth, in the seas, and all deep places. (d) He joined God's power with his will, to the intent that we should not separate them and by this he wills God's people to depend on his power which he confirms by examples.
