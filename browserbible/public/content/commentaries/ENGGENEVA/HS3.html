2 Shall horses {n} run upon the rock? will [one] plow [there] with oxen? for ye have turned judgment into gall, and the fruit of righteousness into {o} hemlock: (n) He compares them to barren rocks, upon which it is in vain to bestow labour: showing that God's benefits can have no place among them. (o) Read (Am 5:7).
3 Ye which rejoice in a thing of nought, which say, Have we not taken to us {p} horns by our own strength? (p) That is, power and glory.
4 But, behold, I will raise up against you a nation, O house of Israel, saith the LORD the God of hosts; and they shall afflict you from the entering in of {q} Hemath unto the river of the wilderness. (q) From one corner of the country to another.
