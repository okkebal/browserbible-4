2 {p} The steps of a [good] man are ordered by the LORD: and he delighteth in his way. (p) God prospers the faithful because they walk in his ways with an upright conscience.
3 Though he {q} fall, he shall not be utterly cast down: for the LORD upholdeth [him with] his hand. (q) When God exercises his faith with various temptations.
4 I have been young, and [now] am old; yet have I not seen the righteous forsaken, nor his {r} seed begging bread. (r) Though the just man die, yet God's blessings are extended to his posterity and though God suffer some just man to lack temporal benefits, yet he recompenses him with spiritual treasures.
8 The righteous shall inherit the land, and dwell therein {s} for ever. (s) They will continually be preserved under God's wings, and have at least inward rest.
9 The {t} mouth of the righteous speaketh wisdom, and his tongue talketh of judgment. (t) These three points are required from the faithful, that their talk is godly, that God's law is in their heart, and that their life is upright.
