1 Surely I have behaved and quieted myself, as a child that is weaned of his mother: my soul [is] even as a {c} weaned child. (c) He was void of ambition and wicked desires.
4 A Song of degrees. LORD, remember David, [and] all his {a} afflictions: (a) That is, with great difficulty he came to the kingdom, and with great zeal and care he went about to build your temple.
6 Surely I {b} will not come into the tabernacle of my house, nor go up into my bed; (b) Because the chief charge of the king was to set forth God's glory, he shows that he would take no rest, neither would he go about any worldly thing, were it never so necessary before he had executed his office.
8 Until I find out a place for the {c} LORD, an habitation for the mighty [God] of Jacob. (c) That is, the ark, which was a sign of God's presence.
9 Lo, we heard of it at {d} Ephratah: we found it in the fields of the wood. (d) The common instinct was that the ark should remain in Ephratah, that is, in Bethlehem a plentiful place: but later we perceived that you would place it in Jerusalem, which was barren as a forest and compassed about only with hills.
