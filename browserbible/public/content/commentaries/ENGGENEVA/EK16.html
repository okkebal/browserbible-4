5 With the hoofs of his horses shall he tread down all thy streets: he shall slay thy people by the sword, and thy strong {e} garrisons shall go down to the ground. (e) For Tyre was built by art and by labour of men was won out of the sea. Some refer this to the image of the noble men which they had erected for their glory and renown.
8 And I will make thee like the top of a rock: {f} thou shalt be [a place] to spread nets upon; thou shalt be built no more: for I the LORD have spoken [it], saith the Lord GOD. (f) I will make you so bare that you will have nothing to cover you.
10 Then all the princes of the {g} sea shall come down from their thrones, and lay away their robes, and put off their embroidered garments: they shall clothe themselves with trembling; they shall sit upon the ground, and shall tremble at [every] moment, and be astonished at thee. (g) The governors and rulers of other countries that dwell by the sea: by which he signifies that her destruction would be so horrible that all the world would hear of it and be afraid.
11 And they shall take up a lamentation for thee, and say to thee, How art thou destroyed, [that wast] inhabited {h} by seafaring men, the renowned city, which wast strong in the sea, she and her inhabitants, who cause their terror [to be] on all that dwelt in it! (h) Meaning, merchants who by their traffic enriched her wonderfully and increased her power.
14 When I shall bring thee down with them that descend into the pit, with the people {o} of old time, and shall set thee in the low parts of the earth, in places desolate of old, with them that go down to the pit, that thou be not inhabited; and I shall set glory in the land of the {k} living; (i) Who were dead long ago. (k) Meaning in Judea when it will be restored.
19 And say to Tyre, O thou that dwelleth at the entrance of the sea, [which art] a merchant {a} of the people for many isles, Thus saith the Lord GOD; O Tyre, thou hast said, I [am] of perfect beauty. (a) Which serves all the world with your merchandise.
21 They have made all thy [ship] planks of fir trees of {b} Senir: they have taken cedars from Lebanon to make masts for thee. (b) This mountain was called Hermon but the Amorites called it Shenir, (De 3:9).
22 [Of] the oaks of Bashan have they made thy oars; the company of the Ashurites have made thy benches [of] ivory, [brought] out of the isles of {c} Chittim. (c) Which is taken for Greece and Italy.
25 The ancients of Gebal and its wise [men] were in thee thy {d} calkers: all the ships of the sea with their mariners were in thee to exchange thy merchandise. (d) Meaning, that they built the walls of the city, which is here meant by the ship: and of these were the builders of Solomon's temple, (1Ki 5:18).
27 The men of Arvad with thy army [were] upon thy walls on all sides, and the {e} Gammadims were in thy towers: they hung their shields upon thy walls on every side; they have made thy beauty perfect. (e) That is they of Cappadocia, or pygmies and dwarfs which were called because from the high towers they seemed little.
29 {f} Javan, Tubal, and Meshech, they [were] thy merchants: they traded {g} in the persons of men and vessels of brass in thy market. (f) Of Greece, Italy and Cappadocia. (g) By selling slaves.
30 They of the house of {h} Togarmah traded in thy fairs with horses and horsemen and mules. (h) Which are taken for a people of Asia minor.
31 The men of Dedan [were] thy merchants; many isles [were] the merchandise of thy hand: they brought thee [for] a present {i} horns of ivory and ebony. (i) Meaning, unicorn's horns and elephant's teeth.
33 Judah, and the land of Israel, they [were] thy merchants: they traded in thy market in wheat of {k} Minnith, and Pannag, and honey, and oil, and balm. (k) Where the best wheat grew.
42 Thy rowers have brought thee into great waters: the {l} east wind hath broken thee in the midst of the seas. (l) That is, Nebuchadnezzar.
44 The {m} common lands shall shake at the sound of the cry of thy pilots. (m) That is, the cities near you, as Zidon, Arund and others.
52 The merchants among the people shall hiss at thee; thou shalt be a terror, and never [shalt be] {n} any more. (n) By which is meant a long time: for it was prophesied to be destroyed but seventy years, (Isa 23:15).
54 The merchants among the people shall hiss at thee; thou shalt be a terror, and never [shalt be] {n} any more. (n) By which is meant a long time: for it was prophesied to be destroyed but seventy years, (Isa 23:15).
55 Son of man, say to the prince of Tyre, Thus saith the Lord GOD; Because thy heart [is] lifted up, and thou hast said, {a} I [am] a god, I sit [in] the seat of God, in the midst of the seas; yet thou [art] a man, and not God, though thou settest thy heart as the heart of God: (a) I am safe as God is safe in the heavens and no one can hurt me.
56 Behold, thou [art] wiser than {b} Daniel; there is no secret that they can hide from thee: (b) Thus he speaks by derision: for Daniel had declared notable signs of his wisdom in Babylon, when Ezekiel wrote this.
63 Thou shalt die the deaths of the {c} uncircumcised by the hand of strangers: for I have spoken [it], saith the Lord GOD. (c) Like the rest of the heathen and infidels who are God's enemies.
