2 For my days are {c} consumed like smoke, and my bones are burned as an hearth. (c) These excessive kinds of speech show how much the affliction of the Church should wound the hearts of the godly.
3 My heart is smitten, and withered like grass; so that I forget {d} to eat my bread. (d) My sorrows were so great that I did not eat.
5 I am like a {e} pelican of the wilderness: I am like an owl of the desert. (e) Always mourning in solitude and casting out fearful cries.
7 Mine enemies reproach me all the day; [and] they that are mad against me are {f} sworn against me. (f) Have conspired my death.
8 For I have {g} eaten ashes like bread, and mingled my drink with weeping, (g) I have not risen out of my mourning to take my refreshment.
9 Because of thine {h} indignation and thy wrath: for thou hast lifted me up, and cast me down. (h) He shows that not only the afflictions moved him, but chiefly the feeling of God's displeasure.
11 But thou, O LORD, shalt {i} endure for ever; and thy remembrance unto all generations. (i) Though we are frail, yet your promise is sure, and the remembrance of it will confirm us forever.
12 Thou shalt arise, [and] have mercy upon Zion: for the time to favour her, yea, the {k} set time, is come. (k) That is, the seventy years which by the prophet Jeremiah you appointed, (Jer 29:12).
13 For thy servants take pleasure in her {l} stones, and favour the dust thereof. (l) The more the Church is in misery and desolation, the more the faithful should love and pity it.
15 When the LORD shall build up Zion, he shall appear {m} in his glory. (m) That is, when he will have drawn his church out of the darkness of death.
17 This shall be written for the generation to come: and the people which shall be {n} created shall praise the LORD. (n) The deliverance of the Church is an excellent benefit, and therefore he compares it to a new creation for in their banishment the body of the Church seemed to have been dead, which by deliverance was as it were created anew.
19 To hear the groaning of the prisoner; to loose those that are {o} appointed to death; (o) Who now in their banishment could look for nothing but death.
