1 {k} He teareth [me] in his wrath, who hateth me: he gnasheth upon me with his teeth; mine enemy sharpeneth his eyes upon me. (k) That is, God by his wrath: and in this diversity of words and high style, he expresses how grievous the hand of God was on him.
2 They have gaped upon me with their mouth; they have smitten me upon the {l} cheek reproachfully; they have gathered themselves together against me. (l) That is, has handled me contemptuously: for so slapping the cheek signified, (1Ki 22:24, Mr 14:65)
3 God hath delivered me to the ungodly, and turned me over into the {m} hands of the wicked. (m) They have led me where they would.
5 His {n} archers compass me round about, he cleaveth my reins asunder, and doth not spare; he poureth out my gall {o} upon the ground. (n) His manifold afflictions. (o) I am wounded to the heart.
7 I have sewed sackcloth upon my skin, and defiled my {p} horn in the dust. (p) Meaning, his glory was brought low.
9 Not for [any] injustice in {q} mine hands: also my prayer {r} [is] pure. (q) Signifying that he is not able to understand the cause of this his grievous punishment. (r) That is, unfeigned and without hypocrisy.
10 O earth, cover not thou my {s} blood, and let my cry have no place. (s) Let my sin be known if I am such a sinner as my adversaries accuse me, and let me find no favour.
11 Also now, behold, my {t} witness [is] in heaven, and my record [is] on high. (t) Though man condemn me, yet God is witness of my cause.
12 My friends {u} scorn me: [but] mine eye poureth out [tears] unto God. (u) Use painted words instead of true consolation.
13 O that one might plead for a man with God, as a man {x} [pleadeth] for his neighbour! (x) Thus by his great torments he is carried away, and breaks out into passions, and speaks unadvisedly, as though God would intreat man more gently, seeing he has only a short time here to live.
17 [Are there] not {a} mockers with me? and doth not mine eye continue in {b} their provocation? (a) Instead of comfort, being now at death's door, he had but them that mocked at him, and discouraged him.
18 {c} Lay down now, put me in a surety with thee; who [is] he [that] {d} will strike hands with me? (c) He reasons with God as a man beside himself, so that his cause might be brought to light. (d) And answers for you?
19 For thou hast hid their heart from {e} understanding: therefore shalt thou not exalt [them]. (e) That these my afflictions are your just judgments, though man does not know the reason.
20 {f} He that speaketh flattery to [his] friends, even the eyes of his children shall fail. (f) He who flatters a man, and only judges him happy in his prosperity, will not himself only but in his posterity be punished.
21 He hath made me also a {g} byword of the people; and aforetime I was as a tabret. (g) God has made all the world speak of me, because of my afflictions.
23 Upright [men] shall be astonied at {i} this, and the innocent shall stir up himself against the hypocrite. (i) That is, when they see the godly punished: but in the end they will come to understanding and know what will be the reward of the hypocrite.
24 The righteous also shall hold on his {k} way, and he that hath clean hands shall be stronger and stronger. (k) That is, will not be discouraged, considering that the godly are punished as well as the wicked.
25 But as for {l} you all, do ye return, and come now: for I cannot find [one] wise [man] among you. (l) Job speaks to the three who came to comfort him.
27 They change the {m} night into day: the light [is] short because of darkness. (m) That is, have brought me sorrow instead of comfort.
