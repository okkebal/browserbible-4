3 And he turned unto her by the way, and said, Go to, I pray thee, let me come in unto thee; (for he {e} knew not that she [was] his daughter in law.) And she said, What wilt thou give me, that thou mayest come in unto me? (e) God miraculously blinded him so that he could not know her by her voice.
7 And Judah sent the kid by the hand of his {f} friend the Adullamite, to receive [his] pledge from the woman's hand: but he found her not. (f) That his wickedness might not be known to others.
10 And Judah said, Let her take [it] to her, lest we be {g} shamed: behold, I sent this kid, and thou hast not found her. (g) He fears man more than God.
11 And it came to pass about three months after, that it was told Judah, saying, Tamar thy daughter in law hath played the harlot; and also, behold, she [is] with child by whoredom. And Judah said, Bring her forth, and let her be {h} burnt. (h) We see that the Law, which was written in man's heart, taught them that adultery should be punished with death, even though no law had been given yet.
13 And Judah acknowledged [them], and said, She hath been {i} more righteous than I; because that I gave her not to Shelah my son. And he knew her again {k} no more. (i) That is, she ought rather to accuse me than I her. (k) for the horror of the sin condemned him.
16 And it came to pass, as he {l} drew back his hand, that, behold, his brother came out: and she said, How {m} hast thou broken forth? [this] breach [be] upon thee: therefore his name was called Pharez. (l) Their heinous sin was signified by this monstrous birth. (m) Or the separation between you and your brother.
19 And Joseph was brought down to Egypt; and Potiphar, an {a} officer of Pharaoh, captain of the guard, an Egyptian, bought him of the hands of the Ishmeelites, which had brought him down thither. (a) See (Ge 37:36).
20 And the {b} LORD was with Joseph, and he was a prosperous man; and he was in the house of his master the Egyptian. (b) The favour of God is the fountain of all prosperity.
22 And Joseph found grace in his sight, and he served him: and he made him {c} overseer over his house, and all [that] he had he put into his hand. (c) Because God prospered him: and so he made religion serve his profit.
23 And it came to pass from the time [that] he had made him overseer in his house, and over all that he had, that the LORD {d} blessed the Egyptian's house for Joseph's sake; and the blessing of the LORD was upon all that he had in the house, and in the field. (d) The wicked are blessed by the company of the godly.
24 And he left all that he had in Joseph's hand; {e} and he knew not ought he had, save the bread which he did eat. And Joseph was [a] goodly [person], and well favoured. (e) For he was sure that everything would prosper: therefore he ate and drank and did not worry.
25 And it came to pass after these things, that his master's wife cast her eyes upon Joseph; and she said, {f} Lie with me. (f) In this word he declares the purpose she was working towards.
27 [There is] none greater in this house than I; neither hath he kept back any thing from me but thee, because thou [art] his wife: how then can I do this great wickedness, and sin against {g} God? (g) The fear of God preserved him against her continual temptations.
32 That she called unto the men of her house, and spake unto them, saying, See, he hath brought in an Hebrew unto us to mock us; he came in unto me to lie with me, and I {h} cried with a loud voice: (h) This declares that in which lack of restraint exists and to this is joined extreme impudency and deceit.
38 And Joseph's master took him, and put him into the {i} prison, a place where the king's prisoners [were] bound: and he was there in the prison. (i) His bad treatment in the prison may be gathered from (Ps 105:18).
40 And the keeper of the prison committed to Joseph's hand all the prisoners that [were] in the prison; and {k} whatsoever they did there, he was the doer [of it]. (k) That is, nothing was done without his commandment.
45 And he put them in ward in the house of the captain of the guard, into the prison, the place where {a} Joseph [was] bound. (a) God works in many wonderful ways to deliver his own.
47 And they dreamed a dream both of them, each man his dream in one night, {b} each man according to the interpretation of his dream, the butler and the baker of the king of Egypt, which [were] bound in the prison. (b) That is, every dream had his interpretation, as the thing afterward declared.
50 And they said unto him, We have dreamed a dream, and [there is] no interpreter of it. And Joseph said unto them, {c} [Do] not interpretations [belong] to God? tell me [them], I pray you. (c) Cannot God raise up such as shall interpret such things.
