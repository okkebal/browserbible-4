5 Surely I would speak to the Almighty, and I desire {a} to reason with God. (a) For although he knew that God was just, which was revealed in his ordinary working and another in his secret counsel, yet he uttered his affection to God, because he was not able to understand the reason he punished him.
6 But ye [are] forgers of lies, ye [are] all {b} physicians of no value. (b) You do not well apply your medicine to the disease.
9 Will ye speak {c} wickedly for God? and talk deceitfully for him? (c) He condemns their zeal, who did not have knowledge, nor regarded they to comfort him, but always granted on God's justice, as though it was not evidently seen in Job, unless they had undertaken the probation of it.
