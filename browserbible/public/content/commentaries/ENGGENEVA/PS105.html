1 ZAIN. Remember {a} the word unto thy servant, upon which thou hast caused me to hope. (a) Though he feels God's hand still lies on him, yet he rests on his promise, and comforts himself in it.
3 The {b} proud have had me greatly in derision: [yet] have I not declined from thy law. (b) Meaning the wicked who contemn God's word, and tread his religion under foot.
4 I remembered thy {c} judgments of old, O LORD; and have comforted myself. (c) That is, the examples, by which you declare yourself to be judge of the world.
5 {d} Horror hath taken hold upon me because of the wicked that forsake thy law. (d) That is, a vehement zeal to your glory and indignation against the wicked.
6 Thy statutes have been my songs in the house of my {e} pilgrimage. (e) In the course of this life and sorrowful exit.
7 I have remembered thy name, O LORD, in the {f} night, and have kept thy law. (f) Even when others sleep.
8 {g} This I had, because I kept thy precepts. (g) That is, all these benefits.
9 CHETH. [Thou art] my {a} portion, O LORD: I have said that I would keep thy words. (a) I am persuaded that to keep your law is a heritage and great gain for me.
11 I thought on my {b} ways, and turned my feet unto thy testimonies. (b) He shows that no one can embrace the word of God, unless he considers his own imperfections and ways.
13 The bands of the wicked have {c} robbed me: [but] I have not forgotten thy law. (c) They have gone about to draw me into their company.
15 I [am] a {d} companion of all [them] that fear thee, and of them that keep thy precepts. (d) Not only in mutual consent, but also with aid and help.
16 The earth, O LORD, is full of thy mercy: {e} teach me thy statutes. (e) For the knowledge of God's word is a singular token of his favour.
17 TETH. Thou hast dealt {a} well with thy servant, O LORD, according unto thy word. (a) Having proved by experience that God was true in his promise, he desires that he would increase in him knowledge and judgment.
19 Before I was {b} afflicted I went astray: but now have I kept thy word. (b) So Jeremiah says, that before the Lord touched him, he was like a calf untamed so that the use of God's rod is to call us home to God.
22 {c} Their heart is as fat as grease; [but] I delight in thy law. (c) Their heart is indurate and hardened, puffed up with prosperity and vain estimation of themselves.
23 [It is] {d} good for me that I have been afflicted; that I might learn thy statutes. (d) He confesses that before he was chastened he was rebellious as man by nature is.
25 JOD. Thy hands have {a} made me and fashioned me: give me understanding, that I may learn thy commandments. (a) Because God does not leave his work that he has begun, he desires a new grace: that is, that he would continue his mercies.
26 They that {b} fear thee will be glad when they see me; because I have hoped in thy word. (b) When God shows his grace toward any, he testifies to others that he does not fail them who trust in him.
29 Let thy tender mercies come unto me, that I may {c} live: for thy law [is] my delight. (c) He declares that when he did not feel God's mercies, he was as dead.
31 Let those that fear thee {d} turn unto me, and those that have {e} known thy testimonies. (d) That is, be comforted by my example. (e) He shows that there can be no true fear of God without the knowledge of his word.
33 CAPH. My soul {a} fainteth for thy salvation: [but] I hope in thy word. (a) Though my strength fails me, yet my soul groans and sighs, resting still in your word.
35 For I am become like a {b} bottle in the smoke; [yet] do I not forget thy statutes. (b) Like a skin bottle or bladder that is parched in the smoke.
36 How many [are] the {c} days of thy servant? when wilt thou execute judgment on them that persecute me? (c) How long will you afflict your servant.
37 The proud have {d} digged pits for me, which [are] not after thy law. (d) They have not only oppressed me violently but also craftily conspired against me.
38 All thy commandments [are] faithful: they persecute me wrongfully; {e} help thou me. (e) He assures himself that God will deliver his and destroy such as unjustly persecute them.
39 They had almost consumed {f} me upon earth; but I forsook not thy precepts. (f) Finding no help on earth, he lifts up his eyes to heaven.
41 LAMED. For ever, O LORD, thy word is settled in {a} heaven. (a) Because no one should esteem God's word according to the changes of things in this world, he shows that it abides in heaven, and therefore is immutable.
43 They {b} continue this day according to thine ordinances: for all [are] thy servants. (b) Seeing the earth and all creatures remain in that estate in which you have created them, much more your truth remains constant and unchangeable.
