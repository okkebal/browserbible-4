1 [There are] many devices in a {g} man's heart; nevertheless the counsel of the LORD, that shall stand. (g) Man's device will not have success, unless God governs it, whose purpose is unchangeable.
2 The desire of a man [is] his {h} kindness: and a poor man [is] better than a liar. (h) That is, that he be honest: for the poor man who is honest is to be esteemed above the rich who is not virtuous.
5 Smite a scorner, and the {i} simple will beware: and reprove one that hath understanding, [and] he will understand knowledge. (i) That is, the simple and ignorant men learn when they see the wicked punished.
8 An ungodly witness scorneth judgment: and the mouth of the wicked {k} devoureth iniquity. (k) Takes a pleasure and delight in it, as gluttons and drunkards in delicate meats and drinks.
11 {a} Wine [is] a mocker, strong drink [is] raging: and whoever is deceived by it is not wise. (a) By wine here is meant him that is given to wine, and so by strong drink.
12 The fear of a king [is] as the roaring of a lion: [whoever] provoketh him to anger {b} sinneth [against] his own soul. (b) Puts his life in danger.
15 Counsel in the heart of {c} man [is like] deep water; but a man of understanding will draw it out. (c) It is hard to find out: for it is as deep waters, whose bottom cannot be found: yet the wise man will know a man either by his words or manners.
18 A king that sitteth on the throne of judgment {d} scattereth away all evil with his eyes. (d) Where righteous judgment is executed, there sin ceases, and vice dare not appear.
20 Differing weights, [and] differing measures, both of {e} them [are] alike abomination to the LORD. (e) Read (Pr 16:11).
26 Take his {f} garment that is surety [for] a stranger: and take a pledge of him for a strange woman. (f) Teach him wit, that he cast not himself rashly into danger.
35 [It is] a snare to the man [who] {g} devoureth [that which is] holy, and after vows to make enquiry. (g) That is, to apply or take for his own use, that which was appointed to God's and then ask how he may be exempted from the fault.
36 A wise king scattereth the wicked, and bringeth the {h} wheel over them. (h) Which was a kind of punishment then used.
