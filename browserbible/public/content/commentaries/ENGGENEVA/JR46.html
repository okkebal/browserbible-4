1 To subvert a man in his cause, the Lord {q} approveth not. (q) He does not delight in it.
2 Who [is] he [that] saith, and it cometh to pass, [when] the Lord {r} commandeth [it] not? (r) He shows that nothing is done without God's providence.
3 Out of the mouth of the most High proceedeth not {s} evil and good? (s) That is, adversity and prosperity, (Am 3:6).
4 Why doth a living {t} man complain, a man for the punishment of his sins? (t) When God afflicts him.
6 Let us lift up {u} our heart with [our] hands to God in the heavens. (u) That is, both hearts and hands: for else to lift up the hands is but hypocrisy.
16 My eye {x} affecteth my heart because of all the daughters of my city. (x) I am overcome with sore weeping for all my people.
18 They have cut off my life {y} in the dungeon, and cast a stone upon me. (y) Read (Jer 37:16) how he was in the miry dungeon.
23 O Lord, thou hast pleaded the causes of my {z} soul; thou hast redeemed my life. (z) Meaning, the reason his life was in danger.
