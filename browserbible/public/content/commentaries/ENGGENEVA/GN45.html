2 Now therefore be not {b} grieved, nor angry with yourselves, that ye sold me hither: for God did send me before you to preserve life. (b) This example teaches that we must by all means comfort those who are truly ashamed and sorry for their sins.
5 So now [it was] not you [that] sent me hither, but {c} God: and he hath made me a father to Pharaoh, and lord of all his house, and a ruler throughout all the land of Egypt. (c) Though God detests sin, yet he turns man's wickedness into his glory.
9 And, behold, your eyes see, and the eyes of my brother Benjamin, that [it is] {d} my mouth that speaketh unto you. (d) That is, that I speak in your own language and have no interpreter.
15 And take your father and your households, and come unto me: and I will give you the {e} good of the land of Egypt, and ye shall eat the {f} fat of the land. (e) The most plentiful ground. (f) The main fruits and conveniences.
