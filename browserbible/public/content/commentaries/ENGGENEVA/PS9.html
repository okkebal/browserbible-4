2 Give unto the LORD, O ye {a} mighty, give unto the LORD glory and strength. (a) He exhorts the proud tyrants to humble themselves under God's hand, and not to be inferior to brute beasts and dumb creatures.
4 The {b} voice of the LORD [is] upon the waters: the God of glory thundereth: the LORD [is] upon many waters. (b) The thunder claps that are heard out of the clouds should make the wicked tremble for fear of God's anger.
6 The {c} voice of the LORD breaketh the cedars; yea, the LORD breaketh the cedars of Lebanon. (c) That is, the thunderbolt breaks the most strong trees, and will men think their power able to resist God?
7 He maketh them also to skip like a calf; Lebanon and {d} Sirion like a young unicorn. (d) Called also Hermon.
8 The voice of the LORD divideth the {e} flames of fire. (e) It causes the lightnings to shoot and glide.
9 The voice of the LORD shaketh the wilderness; the LORD shaketh the wilderness of {f} Kadesh. (f) In places most desolate, where it seems there is no presence of God.
10 The voice of the LORD maketh the hinds to {g} calve, and {h} discovereth the forests: and in his {i} temple doth every one speak of [his] glory. (g) For fear makes them deliver their calves. (h) Makes the trees bare, or pierces the most secret places. (i) Though the wicked are not moved by these lights, yet the faithful praise God.
11 The LORD sitteth upon the {k} flood; yea, the LORD sitteth King for ever. (k) To moderate the rage of the tempests and waters that they not destroy all.
14 I will extol thee, O LORD; {b} for thou hast lifted me up, and hast not made my foes to rejoice over me. (a) After Absalom had polluted it with most filthy fornication. (b) He condemns them for great ingratitude who do not praise God for his benefits.
15 O LORD my God, I cried unto thee, and thou hast {c} healed me. (c) Restored from the rebellion of Absalom.
16 O LORD, thou hast brought up my {d} soul from the grave: thou hast kept me alive, that I should not go down to the pit. (d) Meaning, that he escaped death most narrowly.
17 Sing unto the LORD, O ye {e} saints of his, and give thanks at the remembrance of his holiness. (e) The word signifies them who have received mercy, and show mercy liberally to others.
19 And in my {g} prosperity I said, I shall never be moved. (g) I put too much confidence in my quiet state as in (Jer 31:18, 2Ch 32:24,25).
20 LORD, by thy favour thou hast made my {h} mountain to stand strong: thou didst hide thy face, [and] I {i} was troubled. (h) I thought you had established me in Zion most surely. (i) After you had withdrawn your help, I felt my misery.
