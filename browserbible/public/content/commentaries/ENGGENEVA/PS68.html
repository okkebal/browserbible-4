4 I should soon have subdued their enemies, and turned my hand {l} against their adversaries. (l) If their sins had not.
5 The haters of the LORD should have submitted themselves unto him: but their time {m} should have endured for ever. (m) If the Israelites had not broken covenant with God, he would have given them victory against their enemies.
6 He should have fed them also with the {n} finest of the wheat: and with honey out of the rock should I have satisfied thee. (n) That is, with most fine wheat and abundance of honey.
8 God standeth in the congregation of the {a} mighty; he judgeth among the gods. (a) The prophet shows that if princes and judges do not do their duty, God whose authority is above them will take vengeance on them.
9 How long will ye judge unjustly, and accept the persons of the {b} wicked? Selah. (b) For thieves and murderers find favour in judgment when the cause of the godly cannot be heard.
11 Deliver the poor and {c} needy: rid [them] out of the hand of the wicked. (c) Not only when they cry for help, but when their cause requires aid and support.
12 They know not, neither will they understand; they walk on in darkness: all the {d} foundations of the earth are out of course. (d) That is, all things are out of order either by their tyranny or careless negligence.
14 {e} But ye shall die like men, and fall like one of the princes. (e) No title of honour will excuse you, but you will be subject to God's judgments, and tender account as well as other men.
15 Arise, O God, judge the earth: for thou shalt inherit {f} all nations. (f) Therefore no tyrant will pluck your right and authority from you.
17 Keep {a} not thou silence, O God: hold not thy peace, and be not still, O God. (a) This psalm seems to have been composed as a form of prayer against the dangers that the Church was in, in the days of Jehoshaphat.
18 For, lo, thine {b} enemies make a tumult: and they that hate thee have lifted up the head. (b) He calls them God's enemies, who are enemies of his Church.
19 They have taken crafty counsel against thy people, and consulted against thy {c} hidden ones. (c) The elect of God are his secret ones: for he hides them in the secret of his tabernacle, and preserves them from all dangers.
20 They have said, Come, and let us {d} cut them off from [being] a nation; that the name of Israel may be no more in remembrance. (d) They were not content to take the Church as prisoner: but sought to utterly destroy it.
21 For they have consulted together {e} with one consent: they are confederate {f} against thee: (e) By all secret means. (f) They thought to have subverted your counsel in which the constancy of the Church was established.
24 Assur also is joined with them: they have holpen the children {g} of Lot. Selah. (g) The wickedness of the Ammonites and Moabites is described in that they provoked these other nations to fight against the Israelites.
25 Do unto them as [unto] the {h} Midianites; as [to] Sisera, as [to] Jabin, at the brook of Kison: (h) By these examples they were confirmed that God would not permit his people to be completely destroyed, (Jud 7:21,4:15).
26 [Which] perished at Endor: they became [as] {i} dung for the earth. (i) Trodden under foot as mire.
28 Who said, Let us take to ourselves the {k} houses of God in possession. (k) That is, Judea: for where his Church is, there he dwells among them.
29 O my God, make them like a {l} wheel; as the stubble before the wind. (l) Because the reprobate could by no means be amended, he prays that they may utterly be destroyed, be unstable and led by all winds.
32 Fill their faces with shame; that they may {m} seek thy name, O LORD. (m) That is, be compelled by your plagues to confess your power.
34 That [men] may {n} know that thou, whose name alone [is] JEHOVAH, [art] the most high over all the earth. (n) Though they do not believe, yet they may prove by experience, that it is in vain to resist against your counsel in establishing your Church.
36 How amiable [are] thy tabernacles, O {a} LORD of hosts! (a) David complains that he cannot have access to the Church of God to make profession of his faith, and to profit in religion.
