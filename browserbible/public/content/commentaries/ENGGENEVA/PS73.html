2 And I will {s} beat down his foes before his face, and plague them that hate him. (s) Though there will always be enemies against God's kingdom yet he promises to overcome them.
3 But my faithfulness and my {t} mercy [shall be] with him: and in my name shall his {u} horn be exalted. (t) I will mercifully perform my promise to him despite his infirmities and offences. (u) His power, glory and estate.
4 I will set his hand also in the sea, and his right hand in the {x} rivers. (x) He will enjoy the land around him.
5 He shall cry unto me, Thou [art] my {y} father, my God, and the rock of my salvation. (y) His excellent dignity will appear in this, that he will be named the son of God and the firstborn in which he is a figure of Christ.
8 His seed also will I make [to endure] {z} for ever, and his throne as the days of heaven. (z) Though for the sins of the people the state of the kingdom decayed, yet God reserved still a root till he had accomplished this promise in Christ.
12 {a} Nevertheless my lovingkindness will I not utterly take from him, nor suffer my faithfulness to fail. (a) Though the faithful answer not in all points to their profession, yet God will not break his covenant with them.
13 My covenant will I not break, nor {b} alter the thing that is gone out of my lips. (b) For God in promising has respect to his mercy, and not to man's power in performing.
16 It shall be established for ever as the moon, and [as] a faithful {c} witness in heaven. Selah. (c) As long as the Sun and Moon endure, they will be witnesses to me of this promise.
18 Thou hast {d} made void the covenant of thy servant: thou hast profaned his {e} crown [by casting it] to the ground. (d) Because of the horrible confusion of things, the prophet complains to God, as though he did not see the performance of his promise and thus discharging his cares on God, he resists doubt and impatience. (e) By this he means the horrible dissipation and tearing of the kingdom which was under Jeroboam, or else by the Spirit of prophecy Ethan speaks of those great miseries which came to pass soon after at the captivity of Babylon.
24 The days of his {f} youth hast thou shortened: thou hast covered him with shame. Selah. (f) He shows that the kingdom fell before it came to perfection or was ripe.
25 How long, {g} LORD? wilt thou hide thyself for ever? shall thy wrath burn like fire? (g) The prophet in joining prayer with his complaint, shows that his faith never failed.
26 Remember {h} how short my time is: wherefore hast thou made all men in vain? (h) Seeing man's life is short, and you have created man to bestow your benefits on him, unless you hasten to help, death will prevent you.
