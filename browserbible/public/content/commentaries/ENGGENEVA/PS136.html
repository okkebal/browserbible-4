17 Go to the {b} ant, thou sluggard; consider her ways, and be wise: (b) If the word of God cannot instruct you, learn from the little ant to labour for yourself and not to burden others.
21 [Yet] a little sleep, a little slumber, {c} a little folding of the hands to sleep: (c) He expresses the nature of the sluggards, who though they sleep long, yet never have enough, but always seek opportunity for more.
