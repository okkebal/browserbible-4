2 When they [were] at the great stone which [is] in Gibeon, Amasa went before them. And Joab's {g} garment that he had put on was girded unto him, and upon it a girdle [with] a sword fastened upon his loins in the sheath thereof; and as he went forth it fell out. (g) Which was his coat, that he used to wear in the wars.
5 And one of Joab's men {h} stood by him, and said, He that favoureth Joab, and he that [is] for David, [let him go] after Joab. (h) He stood by Amasa at Joab's appointment.
8 And he went through all the tribes of Israel unto Abel, and to {i} Bethmaachah, and all the Berites: and they were gathered together, and went also after him. (i) To the city Abel, which was near Bethmaachah.
9 And they came and besieged him in Abel of Bethmaachah, and they cast up a bank against the city, and it stood in the trench: and all the people that [were] with Joab {k} battered the wall, to throw it down. (k) That is, he went about to overthrow it.
12 Then she spake, saying, {l} They were wont to speak in old time, saying, They shall surely ask [counsel] at Abel: and so they ended [the matter]. (l) She shows that the old custom was not to destroy a city before peace was offered, (De 20:10,11).
13 I [am {m} one of them that are] peaceable [and] faithful in Israel: thou seekest to destroy a city and a mother in Israel: why wilt thou swallow up the inheritance of the LORD? (m) She speaks in the name of the city.
15 The {n} matter [is] not so: but a man of mount Ephraim, Sheba the son of Bichri by name, hath lifted up his hand against the king, [even] against David: deliver him only, and I will depart from the city. And the woman said unto Joab, Behold, his head shall be thrown to thee over the wall. (n) Hearing his fault, he gave in to reason, and required only he that was instigator of the treason.
20 And Ira also the Jairite was a {o} chief ruler about David. (o) Either in dignity or familiarity.
22 Then there was a famine in the days of David three years, year after year; and David enquired of the LORD. And the LORD answered, [It is] for Saul, and for [his] bloody house, because he slew the {a} Gibeonites. (a) Thinking to gratify the people, because these were not of the seed of Abraham.
