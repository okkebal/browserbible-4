2 For she hath cast down many wounded: yea, many {i} strong [men] have been slain by her. (i) Neither wit nor strength can deliver them who fall into the hands of the harlot.
5 Doth not {a} wisdom cry? and understanding put forth her voice? (a) Solomon declares that man is the cause of his own perdition and that he cannot pretend ignorance, for God calls all men by his word and his works to follow virtue and flee from vice.
7 She crieth at {b} the gates, at the entry of the city, at the entrance of the doors. (b) Where the people resorted most and which was the place of justice.
13 They [are] all {c} plain to him that understandeth, and right to them that find knowledge. (c) Meaning that the word of God is easy to all that have a desire for it and are not blinded by the prince of this world.
