1 And one of the same place answered and said, But who [is] their {e} father? Therefore it became a proverb, [Is] Saul also among the {f} prophets? (e) Meaning, that prophecy comes not by succession, but is given to whom it pleases God. (f) Noting by it him that from low degree comes suddenly to honour.
6 And Samuel {g} called the people together unto the LORD to Mizpeh; (g) Both to declare to them their fault in asking a king, and also to show God's sentence in it.
9 And when Samuel had caused all the tribes of Israel to come near, the tribe of Benjamin was {h} taken. (h) That is, by casting of lot.
11 Therefore they enquired of the LORD further, if the man should yet come thither. And the LORD answered, Behold, he {i} hath hid himself among the stuff. (i) As though he were unworthy and unwilling.
14 Then Samuel told the people {k} the manner of the kingdom, and wrote [it] in a book, and laid [it] up before the LORD. And Samuel sent all the people away, every man to his house. (k) As it is written in (De 17:15).
16 But the children of Belial said, How shall this man save us? And they despised him, and brought him no presents. But he {l} held his peace. (l) Both to avoid disputing and also to win them by patience.
18 Then Nahash the Ammonite {a} came up, and encamped against Jabeshgilead: and all the men of Jabesh said unto Nahash, Make a covenant with us, and we will serve thee. (a) After that Saul was chosen king: for fear of whom they asked a king, as in (1Sa 12:12).
19 And Nahash the Ammonite answered them, On this [condition] will I make [a covenant] with you, that I may thrust out all your {b} right eyes, and lay it [for] a reproach upon all Israel. (b) This declares that the closer the tyrants are to their destruction, the more cruel they are.
