4 And they shall be unto you cities for refuge from the {d} avenger; that the manslayer die not, until he stand before the congregation in judgment. (d) Meaning, from the next of the kindred, who ought to pursue the cause.
6 Ye shall give three cities {e} on this side Jordan, and three cities shall ye give in the land of Canaan, [which] shall be cities of refuge. (e) Among the Reubenites, Gadites, and half the tribe of Manasseh, (De 4:41).
8 And if he {f} smite him with an instrument of iron, so that he die, he [is] a murderer: the murderer shall surely be put to death. (f) Wittingly, and willingly.
9 And if he smite him with throwing a {g} stone, wherewith he may die, and he die, he [is] a murderer: the murderer shall surely be put to death. (g) That is, with a big and dangerous stone: in Hebrew, with a stone of his hand.
16 Then the congregation shall judge between the slayer and the {h} revenger of blood according to these judgments: (h) That is, his next kinsman.
17 And the congregation shall deliver the slayer out of the hand of the revenger of blood, and the congregation shall restore him to the city of his refuge, whither he was fled: and he shall abide in it unto the death of the {i} high priest, which was anointed with the holy oil. (i) Under this figure is declared, that our sins could not be remitted, but by the death of the high Priest Jesus Christ.
19 And the revenger of blood find him without the borders of the city of his refuge, and the revenger of blood kill the {k} slayer; he shall not be guilty of blood: (k) By the sentence of the judge.
21 So these [things] shall be for a {l} statute of judgment unto you throughout your generations in all your dwellings. (l) A law to judge murders done either on purpose, or accidentally.
23 Moreover ye shall take no satisfaction for the life of a murderer, which [is] {m} guilty of death: but he shall be surely put to death. (m) Who purposely committed murder.
25 So ye shall not pollute the land wherein ye [are]: for blood it defileth the land: and the land cannot be {n} cleansed of the blood that is shed therein, but by the blood of him that shed it. (n) So God is mindful of the blood wrongfully shed, that he makes his dumb creatures demand vengeance of it.
28 And the {a} chief fathers of the families of the children of Gilead, the son of Machir, the son of Manasseh, of the families of the sons of Joseph, came near, and spake before Moses, and before the princes, the chief fathers of the children of Israel: (a) It seems that the tribes contended who might marry these daughters to have their inheritance: and therefore the sons of Joseph proposed the matter to Moses.
29 And they said, The LORD commanded {b} my lord to give the land for an inheritance by lot to the children of Israel: and my lord was commanded by the LORD to give the inheritance of Zelophehad our brother unto his daughters. (b) Meaning Moses.
31 And when the {c} jubile of the children of Israel shall be, then shall their inheritance be put unto the inheritance of the tribe whereunto they are received: so shall their inheritance be taken away from the inheritance of the tribe of our fathers. (c) Signifying that at no time could it return, for in the Jubile all things returned to their own tribes.
32 And Moses commanded the children of Israel according to the word of the LORD, saying, The tribe of the sons of Joseph hath said {d} well. (d) For the tribe could not have continued, if the inheritance which was the maintenance of it should have been alienated to others.
35 And every daughter, that possesseth an {e} inheritance in any tribe of the children of Israel, shall be wife unto one of the family of the tribe of her father, that the children of Israel may enjoy every man the inheritance of his fathers. (e) When there is no male to inherit.
40 These [are] the {f} commandments and the judgments, which the LORD commanded by the hand of Moses unto the children of Israel in the plains of Moab by Jordan [near] Jericho. (f) Concerning the ceremonial and judicial laws.
