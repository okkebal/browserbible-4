2 Then let him {n} count the years of the sale thereof, and restore the overplus unto the man to whom he sold it; that he may return unto his possession. (n) Deducting money for the years past, and paying for the rest of the years to come.
3 But if he be not able to restore [it] to him, then that which is sold shall remain in the hand of him that hath bought it until the year of jubile: and in the jubile it shall go {o} out, and he shall return unto his possession. (o) From his hand that bought it.
5 And if it be not redeemed within the space of a full year, then the house that [is] in the walled city shall be established {p} for ever to him that bought it throughout his generations: it shall not go out in the jubile. (p) That is, for ever, read (Le 25:23).
9 But the field of the {q} suburbs of their cities may not be sold; for it [is] their perpetual possession. (q) Where the Levites kept their cattle.
10 And if thy brother be waxen poor, and {r} fallen in decay with thee; then thou shalt relieve him: [yea, though he be] a stranger, or a sojourner; that he may live with thee. (r) In Hebrew it is, if his hand shake: meaning if he stretch forth his hand for help as one in misery.
17 For they [are] my servants, which I brought forth out of the land of Egypt: they shall not {s} be sold as bondmen. (s) To perpetual servitude.
20 Moreover of the children of the strangers that do sojourn among you, of them shall ye buy, and of their families that [are] with you, which they begat in your land: and they shall be your {t} possession. (t) For they shall not be bought out at the Jubile.
24 Either his uncle, or his uncle's son, may redeem him, or [any] that is nigh of kin unto him of his family may redeem him; or {u} if he be able, he may redeem himself. (u) If he be able.
25 And he shall reckon with him that bought him from the year that he was sold to him unto the year of jubile: and the price of his sale shall be according unto the number of {x} years, according to the time of an hired servant shall it be with him. (x) Which remains yet to the Jubile.
28 [And] as a yearly hired servant shall he be with him: [and the other] shall not rule with rigour over him in thy {y} sight. (y) You shall not allow him to treat him severely, if you know it.
