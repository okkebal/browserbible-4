2 And David said unto him, {f} Thy blood [be] upon thy head; for thy mouth hath testified against thee, saying, I have slain the LORD'S anointed. (f) You are justly punished for your fault.
4 (Also he bade them teach the children of Judah {g} [the use of] the bow: behold, [it is] written in the book of Jasher.) (g) That they might be able to match their enemies the Philistines in that art.
5 The beauty of Israel is {h} slain upon thy high places: how are the mighty fallen! (h) Meaning Saul.
7 Ye mountains of Gilboa, [let there be] no dew, neither [let there be] rain, upon you, nor {i} fields of offerings: for there the shield of the mighty is vilely cast away, the shield of Saul, [as though he had] not [been] anointed with oil. (i) Let their fertile fields be barren, and bring forth no fruit to offer to the Lord.
9 Saul and Jonathan [were] lovely and pleasant in their lives, and in their death they were not {k} divided: they were swifter than eagles, they were stronger than lions. (k) They died both together in Gilboa.
10 Ye daughters of Israel, weep over Saul, who clothed you in scarlet, {l} with [other] delights, who put on ornaments of gold upon your apparel. (l) As rich garments and costly jewels.
12 I am distressed for thee, my brother Jonathan: very pleasant hast thou been unto me: thy love to me was wonderful, passing the love of {m} women. (m) Either toward their husbands or their children.
15 And it came to pass after this, that David {a} enquired of the LORD, saying, Shall I go up into any of the cities of Judah? And the LORD said unto him, Go up. And David said, Whither shall I go up? And he said, Unto {b} Hebron. (a) By means of the high priest, (1Sa 23:2, 2Sa 5:19). (b) Which was also called Kirjatharba (Jos 14:15).
17 And his men that [were] with {c} him did David bring up, every man with his household: and they dwelt in the cities of Hebron. (c) In the time of his persecution.
20 And now the LORD shew kindness and {d} truth unto you: and I also will requite you this kindness, because ye have done this thing. (d) According to his promise, which is to repay those who are merciful.
21 Therefore now let your hands be strengthened, and be ye valiant: for your master Saul is dead, and also the house of Judah have anointed me {e} king over them. (e) So that you shall not want a captain and a defender.
23 And made him king over Gilead, and over the Ashurites, and over Jezreel, and over Ephraim, and over Benjamin, and over {f} all Israel. (f) Over the eleven tribes.
25 And the time that David was king in Hebron over the house of Judah was seven years and six {g} months. (g) After this he reigned over all the country 33 years, (2Sa 5:5).
