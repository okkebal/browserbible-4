1 God hath spoken in his {e} holiness; I will rejoice, I will divide Shechem, and mete out the valley of Succoth. (e) As he has spoken to Samuel concerning me, so will he show himself constant and holy in his promise, so that these nations following will be subject to me.
5 {f} [Wilt] not [thou], O God, [who] hast cast us off? and wilt not thou, O God, go forth with our hosts? (f) From the sixth verse of this psalm to the last, read also (Ps 60:5).
9 Hold not thy peace, O God of my {a} praise; (a) Though all the world condemn me, yet you will approve my innocence and that is sufficient praise to me.
12 For my love they are my adversaries: {b} but I [give myself unto] prayer. (b) To declare that I had no other refuge, but you, in whom my conscience was at rest.
14 {c} Set thou a wicked man over him: and let Satan stand at his right hand. (c) Whether it was Doeg or Saul, or some familiar friend that had betrayed him, he prays not for private affection, but moved by God's Spirit, that God would take vengeance on him.
15 When he shall be judged, let him be condemned: and let his {d} prayer become sin. (d) As to the elect all things turn to their profit, so to the reprobate, even those things that are good, turn to their damnation.
16 Let his days be few; [and] let another take his {e} office. (e) This was chiefly accomplished in Judas, (Ac 1:20).
