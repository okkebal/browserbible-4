2 And told him, saying, Joseph [is] yet alive, and he [is] governor over all the land of Egypt. And Jacob's heart {h} fainted, for he believed them not. (h) As one between hope and fear.
6 And Israel took his journey with all that he had, and came to Beersheba, and {a} offered sacrifices unto the God of his father Isaac. (a) By this he signified both that he worshipped the true God, and that he kept in his heart the possession of that land from which need drove him at that time.
9 I will {b} go down with thee into Egypt; and I will also surely {c} bring thee up [again]: and Joseph shall {d} put his hand upon thine eyes. (b) Conducting you by my power. (c) In your posterity. (d) Shall shut your eyes when you die: which belongs to him that was most dear or chief of the kindred.
