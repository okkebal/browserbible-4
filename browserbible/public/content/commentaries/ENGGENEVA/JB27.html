1 Out of the south cometh the whirlwind: and cold out of the {f} north. (f) In Hebrew it is called the scattering wind, because it drives away the clouds and purges the air.
2 By the breath of God frost is given: and the breadth of the waters {g} is straitened. (g) That is, frozen up and dried.
3 Also by watering he {h} wearieth the thick cloud: he scattereth his {i} bright cloud: (h) Gather the vapours and move to and fro to water the earth. (i) That is, the cloud that has lightning in it.
5 He causeth it to come, whether for {k} correction, or for his land, or for mercy. (k) Rain, cold, heat, tempests and such like are sent from God, either to punish man, or to profit the earth, or to declare his favour toward man, as in (Job 36:31).
7 Dost thou know when God disposed them, and caused the {l} light of his cloud to shine? (l) That is, the lightning to break forth in the clouds?
8 Dost thou know the {m} balancings of the clouds, the wondrous works of him which is perfect in knowledge? (m) Which is sometimes changed into rain, or snow, hail or such like.
9 How thy garments [are] {n} warm, when he quieteth the earth by the south [wind]? (n) Why your clothes should keep you warm when the south wind blows rather than when any other wind blows?
10 Hast thou with him spread out the sky, [which is] strong, [and] as a molten looking {o} glass? (o) For the clearness.
11 Teach us what we shall say unto him; [for] we cannot order [our speech] by reason of {p} darkness. (p) That is, our ignorance: signifying that Job was so presumptuous, that he would control the works of God.
12 Shall it be {q} told him that I speak? if a man speak, surely he shall be swallowed up. (q) Has God need that any should tell him when man murmurs against him?
13 And now [men] see not the bright light {s} which [is] in the clouds: but the wind passeth, and cleanseth them. (s) The cloud stops the shining of the sun, that man cannot see it till the wind has chased away the cloud: and if man is not able to attain to the knowledge of these things, how much less God's judgments?
14 {t} Fair weather cometh out of the north: with God [is] terrible majesty. (t) In Hebrew, gold, meaning fair weather and clear as gold.
15 [Touching] the Almighty, we cannot find him out: [he is] excellent in power, and in judgment, and in plenty of justice: he will not {u} afflict. (u) Meaning, without cause.
18 Then the LORD answered Job out of the {a} whirlwind, and said, (a) That his words might have greater majesty, and that Job might know with whom he had to do.
19 Who [is] this that {b} darkeneth counsel by words without knowledge? (b) Which by seeking out the secret counsel of God by man's reason, makes it more obscure, and shows his own folly.
20 Gird up now thy loins like a man; for I {c} will demand of thee, and answer thou me. (c) Because he wished to dispute with God, (Job 23:3), God reasons with him, to declare his rashness.
21 Where wast thou when I {d} laid the foundations of the earth? declare, if thou hast understanding. (d) Seeing he could not judge those things which were done so long before he was born, he was not able to comprehend all God's works: much less the secret causes of his judgments.
