5 And their meat offering and their drink offerings for the bullocks, for the rams, and for the lambs, [shall be] according to their number, after the {g} manner: (g) According to the ceremonies appointed to it.
23 These [things] ye shall do unto the LORD in your set feasts, beside your {h} vows, and your freewill offerings, for your burnt offerings, and for your meat offerings, and for your drink offerings, and for your peace offerings. (h) Beside the sacrifices that you will vow or offer of your own minds.
26 And Moses spake unto the heads of the tribes {a} concerning the children of Israel, saying, This [is] the thing which the LORD hath commanded. (a) Because they might declare them to the Israelites.
29 And her father hear her vow, and her bond wherewith she hath bound her soul, and her father shall hold his {b} peace at her: then all her vows shall stand, and every bond wherewith she hath bound her soul shall stand. (b) For in so doing he approves her.
30 But if her {c} father disallow her in the day that he heareth; not any of her vows, or of her bonds wherewith she hath bound her soul, shall stand: and the LORD shall forgive her, because her father disallowed her. (c) By not approving or consenting to her vow.
31 And if she had at all an husband, when she vowed, or {d} uttered ought out of her lips, wherewith she bound her soul; (d) Either by oath, or solemn promise.
33 But if her husband disallowed her on the day that he heard [it]; then he shall make her vow which she vowed, and that which she uttered with her lips, wherewith she bound her {e} soul, of none effect: and the LORD shall forgive her. (e) For she is in subjection to her husband, and can perform nothing without his consent.
34 But every vow of a widow, and of her that is divorced, wherewith they have bound their souls, shall stand {f} against her. (f) For they are not under the authority of the man.
35 And if she vowed in her husband's {h} house, or bound her soul by a bond with an oath; (h) Her husband being alive.
38 Every vow, and every binding oath to {h} afflict the soul, her husband may establish it, or her husband may make it void. (h) To mortify herself by abstinence or other bodily exercise.
39 But if her husband altogether hold his peace at her from {i} day to day; then he establisheth all her vows, or all her bonds, which [are] upon her: he confirmeth them, because he held his peace at her in the day that he heard [them]. (i) And warn her not the same day that he hears it, as in (Nu 30:8).
40 But if he shall any ways {k} make them void after that he hath heard [them]; then he shall bear her iniquity. (k) Not the same day he heard them, but some day after, the sin will be imputed to him and not to her.
45 And Moses spake unto the people, saying, Arm some of yourselves unto the war, and let them go {a} against the Midianites, and avenge the LORD of Midian. (a) As he had commanded in (Nu 25:17), declaring also that the injury done against his people is done against him.
48 And Moses sent them to the war, a thousand of [every] tribe, them and {b} Phinehas the son of Eleazar the priest, to the war, with the holy instruments, and the trumpets to blow in his hand. (b) For his great zeal that he bare for the Lord, (Nu 25:13).
50 And they slew the kings of Midian, beside the rest of them that were slain; [namely], Evi, and Rekem, and Zur, and Hur, and Reba, five kings of Midian: {c} Balaam also the son of Beor they slew with the sword. (c) The false prophet who gave counsel how to cause the Israelites to offend their God.
54 And they brought the {d} captives, and the prey, and the spoil, unto Moses, and Eleazar the priest, and unto the congregation of the children of Israel, unto the camp at the plains of Moab, which [are] by Jordan [near] Jericho. (d) As the women and little children.
57 And Moses said unto them, Have ye saved all the {e} women alive? (e) As though he said, you should have spared none.
58 Behold, these caused the children of Israel, through the counsel of Balaam, to commit trespass against the LORD {f} in the matter of Peor, and there was a plague among the congregation of the LORD. (f) For worshipping of Peor.
59 Now therefore kill every male among the {g} little ones, and kill every woman that hath known man by lying with him. (g) That is, all the boy children.
65 Every thing that may abide the fire, ye shall make [it] go through the fire, and it shall be clean: nevertheless it shall be {h} purified with the water of separation: and all that abideth not the fire ye shall make go through the {i} water. (h) The third day and before it is molten. (i) It shall be washed.
