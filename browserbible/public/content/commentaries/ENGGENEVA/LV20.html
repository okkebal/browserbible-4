1 Ye shall not eat [any thing] with the blood: neither shall ye use enchantment, nor {i} observe times. (i) To measure lucky or unlucky days.
2 Ye shall not {k} round the corners of your heads, neither shalt thou mar the corners of thy beard. (k) As did the Gentiles in sign of mourning.
3 Ye shall not make any cuttings in your flesh for the dead, nor print any {l} marks upon you: I [am] the LORD. (l) By whipping your bodies or burning marks in them.
4 Do not prostitute thy daughter, to cause her to be a {m} whore; lest the land fall to whoredom, and the land become full of wickedness. (m) As did the Cyprians, and Locrenses.
7 Thou shalt {n} rise up before the hoary head, and honour the face of the old man, and fear thy God: I [am] the LORD. (n) In token of reverence.
10 Ye shall do no unrighteousness in judgment, in {o} meteyard, in weight, or in measure. (o) As in measuring the ground.
11 Just balances, just weights, a just {p} ephah, and a just hin, shall ye have: I [am] the LORD your God, which brought you out of the land of Egypt. (p) By these two measures he means all other. Of Ephah, read (Ex 16:36) and of Hin, (Ex 29:40).
15 Again, thou shalt say to the children of Israel, Whosoever [he be] of the children of Israel, or of the strangers that sojourn in Israel, that giveth [any] of his seed unto {a} Molech; he shall surely be put to death: the people of the land shall stone him with stones. (a) By Molech he means any type of idol, (Le 18:21)
16 And I will {b} set my face against that man, and will cut him off from among his people; because he hath given of his seed unto Molech, to defile my sanctuary, and to profane my holy name. (b) Read (Le 17:10,18:21).
