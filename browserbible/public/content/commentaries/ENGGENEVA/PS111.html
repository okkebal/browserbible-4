1 The LORD shall {d} bless thee out of Zion: and thou shalt see the good of {e} Jerusalem all the days of thy life. (d) Because of the spiritual blessing which God has made to his Church, these temporal things will be granted. (e) For unless God blessed his Church publicly, this private blessing was nothing.
4 Many a time have they afflicted me from my youth, may {a} Israel now say: (a) The Church now afflicted should remember how her condition has always been such from the beginning to be molested most grievously by the wicked, yet in time it has always been delivered.
7 The LORD [is] {b} righteous: he hath cut asunder the cords of the wicked. (b) Because God is righteous, he cannot but plague his adversary, and deliver his as oxen out of the plough.
9 {c} Let them be as the grass [upon] the housetops, which withereth afore it groweth up: (c) The enemies who lift themselves high, and as it were approach the Sun, are consumed with the heat of God's wrath, because they are not grounded in godly humility.
