1 The LORD bringeth the {g} counsel of the heathen to nought: he maketh the devices of the people of none effect. (g) No counsel can prevail against God, but he deserves it, and it will have evil success.
3 Blessed [is] the nation whose {h} God [is] the LORD; [and] the people [whom] he hath chosen for his own inheritance. (h) He shows that all our happiness stands in this, that the Lord is our God.
4 The LORD {i} looketh from heaven; he beholdeth all the sons of men. (i) He proves that all things are governed by God's providence and not by fortune.
