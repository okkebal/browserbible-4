2 As arrows [are] in the hand of a mighty man; so [are] {f} children of the youth. (f) That is, endued with strength and virtues from God: for these are signs of God's blessings, and not the number.
3 Happy [is] the man that hath his quiver full of them: they {g} shall not be ashamed, but they shall speak with the enemies in the gate. (g) Such children will be able to stop their adversaries mouths, when their godly life is maliciously accused before judges.
5 Blessed [is] every one that feareth the LORD; that walketh in his {a} ways. (a) God approves not our life, unless it is reformed according to his word.
6 For thou shalt eat the labour of thine {b} hands: happy [shalt] thou [be], and [it shall be] well with thee. (b) The world esteems them happy who live in wealth and idleness but the Holy Spirit approves them best who live of the mean profit of their labours.
7 Thy wife [shall be] as a fruitful vine by the sides of thine house: thy {c} children like olive plants round about thy table. (c) Because God's favour appears in no outward thing more than in the increase of children, he promises to enrich the faithful with this gift.
