1 Devise not evil against thy neighbour, seeing he dwelleth {n} securely by thee. (n) That is, puts his trust in you.
3 {o} Envy thou not the oppressor, and choose none of his ways. (o) Do not wish to be like him.
4 For the froward [is] abomination to the LORD: but his {p} secret [is] with the righteous. (p) That is, his covenant and fatherly affection which is hid and secret from the world.
6 Surely {q} he scorneth the scorners: but he giveth grace unto the lowly. (q) He will show by his plagues that their scorn will turn to their own destruction, (Pr 1:26).
9 Hear, ye children, the instruction of a {a} father, and attend to know understanding. (a) He speaks this as a preacher and minister which is as a father to the people, (Pr 2:8).
12 He {b} taught me also, and said unto me, Let thine heart retain my words: keep my commandments, and live. (b) Meaning, David his father.
15 {c} Wisdom [is] the principal thing; [therefore] get wisdom: and with all thy getting get understanding. (c) He shows that we must first begin with God's word, if we will that other things prosper with us, contrary to the judgment of the world, which make it their last study, or else care not for it at all.
