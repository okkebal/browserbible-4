3 Now Eli was very old, and heard all that his sons did unto all Israel; and how they lay with the women that {p} assembled [at] the door of the tabernacle of the congregation. (p) Which was (as the Hebrews write) after their travail, when they came to be purified, read (Ex 38:8, Le 12:6).
5 Nay, my sons; for [it is] no good report that I hear: ye make the LORD'S people to {q} transgress. (q) Because they contemn their duty to God, (1Sa 2:17).
6 If one man sin against another, the judge shall judge him: but if a man sin against the LORD, who shall intreat for him? Notwithstanding they hearkened not unto the voice of their father, because the LORD {r} would slay them. (r) So that to obey good admonition is God's mercy, and to disobey them is his just judgment for sin.
8 And there came a man of God unto Eli, and said unto him, Thus saith the LORD, Did I plainly appear unto the house of thy {s} father, when they were in Egypt in Pharaoh's house? (s) That is, Aaron.
10 Wherefore {t} kick ye at my sacrifice and at mine offering, which I have commanded [in my] habitation; and honourest thy sons above me, to make yourselves fat with the chiefest of all the offerings of Israel my people? (t) Why have you contemned my sacrifices, and as it were trod them under foot?
11 Wherefore the LORD God of Israel saith, I said indeed [that] thy house, and the house of thy father, should walk before me for ever: but now the LORD saith, {u} Be it far from me; for them that honour me I will honour, and they that despise me shall be lightly esteemed. (u) God's promises are only effective to those he gives faith to fear and obey him.
12 Behold, the days come, that I will cut off thine {x} arm, and the arm of thy father's house, that there shall not be an old man in thine house. (x) Thy power and authority.
13 And thou {y} shalt see an enemy [in my] habitation, in all [the wealth] which [God] shall give Israel: and there shall not be an old man in thine house for ever. (y) Your posterity will see the glory of the chief priest given to another, whom they will envy, (1Ki 2:27).
16 And I will raise me up a {z} faithful priest, [that] shall do according to [that] which [is] in mine heart and in my mind: and I will build him a sure house; and he shall walk before mine anointed for ever. (z) Meaning Zadok, who succeeded Abiathar, and was the figure of Christ.
17 And it shall come to pass, [that] every one that is left in thine house shall come [and] {a} crouch to him for a piece of silver and a morsel of bread, and shall say, Put me, I pray thee, into one of the priests' offices, that I may eat a piece of bread. (a) That is, will be inferior to him.
19 And the child Samuel ministered unto the LORD {a} before Eli. And the word of the LORD was {b} precious in those days; [there was] no open vision. (a) The Chaldee text reads "while Eli lived". (b) Because there were very few prophets to declare it.
20 And it came to pass at that time, when Eli [was] laid down in his {c} place, and his eyes began to wax dim, [that] he could not see; (c) In the court next to the tabernacle.
21 And ere the {d} lamp of God went out in the temple of the LORD, where the ark of God [was], and Samuel was laid down [to sleep]; (d) That is, the lamps which burnt in the night.
22 That the LORD {e} called Samuel: and he answered, Here [am] I. (e) Josephus writes that Samuel was 12 years old when the Lord appeared to him.
