3 Instead of thy fathers shall be thy {m} children, whom thou mayest make princes {n} in all the earth. (m) They will have greater graces than their fathers. (n) He signifies the great compass of Christ's kingdom, which will be sufficient to enrich all his members.
4 I will make thy {o} name to be remembered in all generations: therefore shall the people praise thee for ever and ever. (o) This must only be referred to Christ and not to Solomon.
6 God [is] our refuge and strength, a very present help in {b} trouble. (a) Which was either a musical instrument or a solemn tune, to which this psalm was sung. (b) In all manner of troubles God shows his speedy mercy and power in defending his.
7 Therefore will not we {c} fear, though the earth be removed, and though the mountains be carried into the midst of the sea; (c) That is, we will not be overcome with fear.
8 [Though] the waters thereof {d} roar [and] be troubled, [though] the mountains shake with the swelling thereof. Selah. (d) Though the afflictions rage, yet the rivers of God's mercies bring sufficient comfort to his.
9 [There is] a {e} river, the streams whereof shall make glad the city of God, the holy [place] of the tabernacles of the most High. (e) The river of Shiloh, which passed through Jerusalem: meaning, though the defence seems small, yet if God has appointed it, it is sufficient.
10 God [is] in the midst of her; she shall not be moved: God shall help her, [and that] {f} right early. (f) Always when need requires.
12 The LORD of hosts [is] {g} with us; the God of Jacob [is] our refuge. Selah. (g) They are assured that God can and will defend his Church from all dangers and enemies.
13 Come, behold the works of the LORD, {h} what desolations he hath made in the earth. (h) That is, how often he has destroyed his enemies, and delivered his people.
