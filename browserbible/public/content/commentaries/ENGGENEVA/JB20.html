1 Did I fear a great multitude, or did the contempt of families {y} terrify me, that I kept {z} silence, [and] went not out of the door? (y) That is, I reverenced the most weak and contemned and was afraid to offend them. (z) I suffered them to speak evil of me, and went not out of my house to avenge it.
2 Oh that one would hear me! behold, my {a} desire [is, that] the Almighty would answer me, and [that] mine adversary had written a book. (a) This is a sufficient token of my righteousness, that God is my witness and will justify my cause.
3 Surely I would take it upon my shoulder, [and] bind it [as] a {b} crown to me. (b) Should not this book of his accusations be a praise and commendation to me?
4 I would declare unto him the number of my steps; as a {c} prince would I go near unto him. (c) I will make him account of all my life, without fear.
5 If my land {d} cry against me, or that the furrows likewise thereof complain; (d) As though I had withheld their wages that laboured in it.
6 If I have eaten the fruits thereof without money, or have caused {e} the owners thereof to lose their life: (e) Meaning, that he was not a briber or extortioner.
7 Let thistles grow instead of wheat, and cockle instead of barley. The {f} words of Job are ended. (f) That is, the talk which he had with his three friends.
9 Let thistles grow instead of wheat, and cockle instead of barley. The {f} words of Job are ended. (f) That is, the talk which he had with his three friends.
10 Then was kindled the wrath of Elihu the son of Barachel the {a} Buzite, of the kindred of {b} Ram: against Job was his wrath kindled, because he justified himself {c} rather than God. (a) Which came from Buz, the son of Nahor, Abraham's brother. (b) Or, as the Chaldee translation reads, Abram. (c) By making himself innocent, and by charging God of rigour.
12 Now Elihu had waited till Job had spoken, because {d} they [were] elder than he. (d) That is, the three mentioned before.
15 I said, Days {e} should speak, and multitude of years should teach wisdom. (e) Meaning, the ancient, who have experience.
16 But [there is] a spirit in man: {f} and the inspiration of the Almighty giveth them understanding. (f) It is a special gift of God that man has understanding and comes neither from nature nor by age.
19 Behold, I waited for your words; I gave ear to your reasons, whilst ye searched out {g} what to say. (g) To prove that Job's affliction came for his sins.
21 Lest ye should say, We have {h} found out wisdom: God thrusteth him down, not man. (h) Flatter yourselves as though you had overcome him.
22 Now {i} he hath not directed [his] words against me: neither will I answer {k} him with your speeches. (i) That is, Job. (k) He uses almost the same arguments but without taunting and reproaches.
26 For I am full of {l} matter, the spirit within me constraineth me. (l) I have conceived in my mind a great store of reasons.
29 Let me not, I pray you, accept any man's person, {m} neither let me give flattering titles unto man. (m) I will neither have regard for riches, credit, nor authority but will speak the very truth.
