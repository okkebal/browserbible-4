PSALM 86

Psa 86:1-17. This is a prayer in which the writer, with deep emotion, mingles petitions and praises, now urgent for help, and now elated with hope, in view of former mercies. The occurrence of many terms and phrases peculiar to David's Psalms clearly intimates its authorship.


1-2. poor and needy--a suffering child of God, as in Psa 10:12, 17; 18:27.
I am holy--or, "godly," as in Psa 4:3; 85:8.


5-7. unto all ... that call upon thee--or, "worship Thee" (Psa 50:15; 91:15) however undeserving (Ex 34:6; Le 11:9-13).


11. Teach--Show, point out.
the way--of Providence.
walk in thy truth--according to its declarations.
unite my heart--fix all my affections (Psa 12:2; Jas 4:8).
to fear thy name--(compare Psa 86:12) to honor Thy perfections.
