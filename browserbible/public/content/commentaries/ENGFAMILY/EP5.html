1 Grieve not the Holy Spirit; by refusing or neglecting to follow his directions.Sealed; marked as the property, and distinguished as the children of God, by the effects which the Holy Spirit produces in you.Redemption; final and complete salvation.
3 Sacred regard to truth; freedom from violent, revengeful, or protracted anger; strict and conscientious integrity; diligence in lawful and useful business; compassion towards the needs, and a disposition to aid them--are all essential to completeness of Christian character: and no one has any more true religion than he has in these respects likeness to Christ.
5 Followers of God; imitators of him, especially in his love to men. We have reason for everlasting gratitude to God that he has given us a perfect example; and it should be our great object perfectly to imitate it.
6 Sweet-smelling savor; peculiarly pleasing and acceptable to God.
7 Covetousness; the excessive desire of worldly gain, leading to its unlawful pursuit.
8 Filthiness; obscenity in words or actions.Not convenient; not fit, proper, useful. A grateful temper, and the habit of expressing it in thanksgiving to God, form a safeguard against temptation and against improprieties of thought, word, and deed.
9 Covetous man; one who regards supremely earthly good. See note to verse (Eph 5:3).
10 Let no man deceive you; by inducing you to think such persons as are mentioned, verse (Eph 5:5), can go to heaven.
11 Those who maintain that continuance in sin will not exclude men from heaven are deceivers. As such they should be treated, and their doctrines be rejected.
12 Darkness; living in ignorance and sin.Light in the Lord; enlightened and renewed by virtue of your union with the Lord Jesus.As children of light; those who belong to the kingdom of light, of which God in Christ is the head.
14 Proving; in an experimental way, by the actual subjection of yourselves to the will of God. See note to (Ro 12:2).
16 It is a shame even to speak of those things; they are too vile to be mentioned or even thought of but with abhorrence.
