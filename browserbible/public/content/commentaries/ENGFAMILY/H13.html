1 Knowing--your election; being chosen of God, of which their reception and treatment of the gospel were evidences.
2 In power, and in the Holy Ghost, and in much assurance; the last clause of this verse shows that the immediate reference of these words is to the apostle and his associates. Their preaching was in power, and in the Holy Ghost, and in full assurance of what they uttered. But this cannot be separated from the effect on their hearers. To them also their preaching was attended with the power of the Holy Ghost; it was embraced with full conviction of its truths, and led them to break off their sins and turn to the Lord. See verse (1Th 1:6).
3 Joy of the Holy Ghost; that which he imparts. A cordial reception of the gospel, and devotion of heart and life to the service of Christ, are sure evidences of being elected, and the pledge of being kept by the power of God through faith unto salvation.
4 Macedonia and Achaia; mentioned together as adjoining Roman provinces, comprising the whole of Greece. Achaia comprised the southern part of Greece, of which Corinth was the capital.
5 The word of the Lord; as manifested in your lives. Verses (1Th 1:9,10).
6 They themselves; the persons named in the preceding verse among whom the word of the Lord sounded out.What manner of entering in we had; what power attended our preaching.
7 Christ is the cause of the deliverance of believers from the wrath to come. This they feel and acknowledge, and to him they give the glory.
9 Christ is the cause of the deliverance of believers from the wrath to come. This they feel and acknowledge, and to him they give the glory.
10 At Philippi; (Ac 16:19-24).Much contention; conflict with opposers, and the dangers and difficulties thence arising.
11 Not of deceit, nor of uncleanness; it did not spring from deceit, nor impure motives.Nor in guile; we had no crafty designs of our own to accomplish under the cloak of preaching the gospel.
12 Allowed of God; approved of God, as the original means.
13 Neither--used we--a cloak of covetousness; they had not used religion to conceal any covetous or selfish purpose.
