1 Thy word - Thy promises.
4 Judgments - Thy ancient dispensations to the children of men in punishing the ungodly.
5 Horror - A mixed passion, made up of abhorrence of their sins, and dread and sorrow at the consideration of the judgments of God coming upon them.
6 The house - In this world, wherein I am a stranger and pilgrim, as all my fathers were.
7 Thy name - Thy holy nature and attributes, thy blessed word, and thy wonderful works. In the night - When darkness causes fear in others, I took pleasure in remembering thee; and when others gave themselves up to sleep, my thoughts and affections were working towards thee.
8 This - This comfortable remembrance.
9 Said - I have professed and owned it.
11 Thought - I seriously considered both my former courses, and my duty in all my future actions.
13 Robbed - Done me many injuries, for my respect to thy law.
18 Judgment - Whereby I may rightly discern between truth and falsehood. Knowledge - A spiritual experimental knowledge.
22 Fat - They are stupid and insensible.
26 Glad - For the encouragement they have by my example to trust in God. See me - Alive and in safety. Word - In thy promise, and have not been disappointed of my hope.
27 Judgments - Thy corrections. Of faithfulness - In pursuance of thy promises, and in order to my good.
31 Known - Loved and practised them.
32 Sound - That I may love and obey them sincerely, constantly, and universally.
33 Fainteth - With long desire and earnest expectation.
35 Bottle - A leathern bottle. My natural moisture is dryed and burnt up.
36 The days - The days of my life. I have but a little while to live; give me some respite before I die.
37 Who - Who have no respect to thy law.
38 Faithful - Just and true.
41 In heaven - With thee in thy heavenly habitation.
43 They - The heaven and the earth. Ordinances - As thou hast appointed. For - All things are subject to thy power and pleasure.
