1 Shall remain - Shall, without repentance, be condemned to eternal death.
2 Wine and oil - Delicious fare and luxurious feasting. Wine and oil were much used in feasts in those parts.
3 A ransom - The wicked shall be brought into those troubles, which were designed by wicked men for the righteous: thus Haman was a ransom for Mordecai.
5 The wise - Wise men lay up desirable treasures for their own use, and for their families. Oil is particularly mentioned, because that was a considerable part of their wealth and treasures in those countries.
7 The strength - The strongest sorts to which the citizens trust.
10 Killeth - Torments him to death, while he passionately desires what he sees, he shall not enjoy, and will not take pains to procure.
11 Coveteth - Spends his whole time in vain and lazy desires. Giveth - By God's blessing upon his industry he procures enough, not only for his own support, but for the relief of others.
12 The sacrifice - All the most costly services which they offer to God. Because - As all the ancient translators render it, the Hebrew particle, aph, being expletive: or, even because; so the following clause gives the reason. A wicked mind - Not in obedience to God's command, but either to cover, or countenance, or promote some wicked design.
13 Heareth - He witnesses nothing but what he has heard or seen. Constantly - Always affirms the same thing.
14 Hardeneth - Continues in evil with obstinacy and impudence. Directs - He orders his steps aright.
16 The horse - Under which all war - like provisions are comprehended.
18 Favour - A good report among men, especially good men, and that hearty kindness which attends it.
19 Meet - They live together, and need one another. The maker - Not only as they are men, but as they are poor or rich, which difference comes from God's providence. They have one common creator, and Lord, and judge, and the one cannot despise nor grudge at the other without reflecting upon God.
20 The evil - The judgment of God threatened, and approaching. Hideth - Retires to his strong tower, by prayer and repentance, puts himself under the protection of the almighty. Pass on - Carefully and securely.
23 Depart - Not easily and ordinarily.
24 Is servant - Is at his mercy, and forced to comply with his pleasure.
25 The rod - That power which he used with fury shall be taken from him.
26 He - Who looks upon the wants and miseries of others with compassion.
29 Preserve - God by the watchful eye of his providence maintains and defends. Knowledge - Men of knowledge, knowing and good men. The matters - All his hopes, enterprizes, and concerns.
31 The mouth - Her fair and flattering speeches.
32 Bound - Is fixed and settled there, as being born with him, and rooted in his very nature.
