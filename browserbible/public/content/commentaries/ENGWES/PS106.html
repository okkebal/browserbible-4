2 Perfection - Of the greatest and most perfect enjoyments in this world. Commandment - Thy word; one part of it being put for the whole. Broad - Or, large, both for extent, and for continuance: it is useful to all persons: it is of everlasting truth and efficacy; it will never deceive those who trust to it, as all worldly things will, but will make men happy both here and for ever.
6 Because - The practice of religion is the best way to understand it.
8 Taught me - By thy blessed spirit, illuminating my mind, and working upon my heart.
14 Offerings - The sacrifices of prayer and praise.
15 Is - Exposed to perpetual danger.
24 Deceit - Their deceitful devices, shall bring that destruction upon themselves which they design for others.
27 Judgment, &c. - Just judgment.
28 Surety - Do thou undertake and plead my cause.
29 The word - For the performance of thy faithful or merciful promise.
32 To work - To put forth thy power for the restraint of evil - doers. They - The wicked. Made void - Or, abrogated thy law, have openly cast off its authority.
33 Therefore - Because the general apostacy of others makes this duty more necessary.
35 Wonderful - In regard of the deep mysteries, the most excellent directions, and the exceeding great and precious promises of God contained in them.
37 Panted - I thirst after thy precepts, and pursue them eagerly.
39 Steps - My motions and actions.
42 Rivers - Plentiful tears, witness of my deep sorrow for God's dishonour, and for the miseries which sinners bring upon themselves.
46 Pure - Without the least mixture of falsehood.
48 Everlasting - The same in all ages and places.
