2 His princes - The princes of Judah. Hanes - An eminent city of Egypt.
3 They - Both the messengers, and they who sent them.
4 The burden - The treasures, which were carried upon asses or camels, into Egypt, which lay southward from Judea. The land of trouble - Egypt, so called prophetically. From whence - This may be understood properly, but withal, seems to design the craft and cruelty of that people. They - The Jews. Their riches - To procure their assistance. Bunches - Upon the backs.
5 To her - To Jerusalem or Judah. Sit still - It is safer and better for them to sit quietly at home, seeking to me for help.
6 Write - This warning. Before - In their presence. Note it - So this was to be written twice over, once in a table, to be hanged up in some public place, that all present might read it; and again, in a book, that it might be kept for the use of posterity. The time to come - As a witness for me and against them.
9 Cause, &c. - Do not trouble us with harsh messages from God.
10 And trust - In the wealth which you have gotten by oppression, and in your perverse course of sending to Egypt for help.
11 This iniquity - Of trusting to Egypt, shall be like a wall which is high, but swelling forth in some parts, which, upon the least accident, falls down suddenly.
12 He - God.
13 In returning - To God. Quietness - In sitting still, and quieting your minds. Confidence - Placed upon me, and my promises.
15 'Till - 'Till you be destroyed, and but a few of you left.
16 Wait - Patiently expect your repentance. Exalted - He will work gloriously. Judgment - Or mercy. That wait - In his way, with faith and patience.
