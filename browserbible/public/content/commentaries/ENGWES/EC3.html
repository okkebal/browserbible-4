2 The great man - Men of all ranks fall down and worship idols.
3 Enter - Such calamities are coming upon you, that you will be ready to hide yourselves in rocks and caves of the earth, for fear of the glorious and terrible judgments of God.
5 The day - The time of God's taking vengeance upon sinners.
6 The cedars - The cedars and oaks on the mountains shall be either thrown down by furious winds or earthquakes, or torn in pieces by thunder and lightning; and the stately houses built with cedars and oaks, shall be destroyed.
7 Hills - To which men used to betake themselves in times of danger.
8 Wall - To which you trusted for your defence.
9 Tarshish - The ships of the sea, as that word is used, (Psal 48:7), whereby you fetched riches from the remote parts of the world.
12 They - The idolatrous Israelites.
13 Shall cast - Into the meanest and darkest places, in which moles and bats have their abode.
15 Cease ye - Never admire or place your trust in man. Breath - Whose breath is quickly stopped and taken away. Wherein - What excellency is in him, considered in himself, and without dependence on God?
17 Cease ye - Never admire or place your trust in man. Breath - Whose breath is quickly stopped and taken away. Wherein - What excellency is in him, considered in himself, and without dependence on God?
18 The judge - The civil magistrates. The ancient - Whose wisdom was increased by long experience.
21 Oppressed - By thy command or permission of such childish rulers.
22 Thou hast - We are utterly undone, and have neither food nor raiment; but thou hast something left to support the dignity which we offer to thee. Under thine hand - To heal it.
