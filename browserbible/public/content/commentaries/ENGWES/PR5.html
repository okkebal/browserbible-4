1 The wise - He who is truly wise, shall be so accounted by others. Sweetness - Eloquence added to wisdom. Increaseth - Both in himself, for while a man teaches others, he improves himself; and others, who by this means are induced to hear and receive his instructions.
2 The instruction - Their most grave and serious counsels are foolish.
3 Teacheth - Directs him what to speak, and keeps him from speaking foolishly. Addeth - Enables him to speak learnedly and wisely.
7 Diggeth up - Prosecutes his evil designs with great industry. His lips - His tongue is set on fire of hell.
10 Shutteth - That his thoughts may be more intent. Moving - Which is the gesture of one whose thoughts are deeply engaged.
12 Is better - Of a more gallant and generous spirit, and more valiant and victorious.
