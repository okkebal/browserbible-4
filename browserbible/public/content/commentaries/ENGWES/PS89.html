3 Soon - Even within three days, (Exod 15:22),23. Waited not - They did not wait patiently upon God for supplies, in such manner and time as he thought fit.
4 Lusted - For flesh.
5 Souls - Into their bodies. So their inordinate desire of pampering their bodies, was the occasion of destroying them.
6 The saint - So called, because he was consecrated by God for that sacred office of the priesthood, in which respect all the priests are said to be holy, (Lev 21:6 )- 8. Hereby he intimates, that their envy and rebellion was not only against Aaron, but against God himself.
9 A calf - When they were but just brought out of Egypt by such wonders, and had seen the plagues of God upon the Egyptian idolaters, and when the law of God was but newly delivered to them in such a tremendous manner.
10 Their glory - God, who was indeed their glory. Into - Into the golden image of an ox or calf, which is so far from feeding his people, as the true God did the Israelites, that he must be fed by them.
13 Breach - God had made a wall about them; but they had made a breach in it by their sins, at which the Lord, who was now justly become their enemy, might enter to destroy them; which he had certainly done, if Moses by his prevailing intercession had not hindered him.
14 Despised - Preferring Egypt, and their former bondage, before it, (Numb 14:3),4.
15 The voice - To God's command, that they should boldly enter into it.
16 Lifted up - He sware. Of this dreadful and irrevocable oath of God, see (Numb 14:11),12.
17 Overthrow - He sware also (tho' not at the same time) that he would punish their sins, not only in their persons, but in their posterity.
18 Joined - They had communion with him, as God's people have with God in acts of his worship.
21 And - It was accepted and rewarded of God as an act of justice and piety.
27 Devils - They did not worship God as they pretended, but devils in their idols; for those spirits, which were supposed by the Heathen idolaters to inhabit in their images, were not good spirits, but evil spirits, or devils.
33 Counsel - By forsaking God's way, and following their own inventions.
35 Repented - Changed his course and dealing with them.
42 Gathered - Into their own land.
43 No city - Or rather, no town inhabited, where they might refresh themselves.
45 The Lord - Heb. Unto Jehovah, to the true God. For the Heathens had, many of them, some knowledge of the true God.
46 Forth - Out of the wilderness.
49 Darkness - In dark prisons or dungeons.
51 Heart - The pride and obstinacy of their hearts. Fell - They fell into hopeless miseries.
