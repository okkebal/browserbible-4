1 The crib - The stable is empty of food for cattle, and much more for man. In the same sense cleanness of teeth is put for famine, (Amos 4:6). The strength - By their labours, or by diligence in husbandry, which then was principally managed by oxen.
3 Findeth it not - Because he doth not seek it aright. That understandeth - That is honest and pious.
5 His way - Consists not in vain speculations, but in a diligent study of his duty, and of the way to eternal happiness. The folly - The wit of ungodly men, which is really their folly, is employed only in finding out ways of deceiving.
6 Favour - They find favour both with God and men.
7 Bitterness - The inward griefs and joys of mens hearts, are not known to any but a man's self.
9 Right - There are some evil courses which men may think to be lawful and good. The end - The event shews that they were sinful and destructive.
10 In laughter - The outward signs of joy are often mixed with real sorrow.
11 In heart - He who departs from God inwardly. Filled - With the fruit of his ways, the punishment of his sins. Satisfied - From the pious temper of his own heart, which cleaves to the Lord, he shall receive unspeakable comfort.
12 The simple - A foolish man. Looketh - He orders his dealings in the world with due circumspection, not considering so much what other men say as what he ought to do.
13 Feareth - Trembles at God's judgments when they are either inflicted or threatened. Rageth - Frets against God; or is enraged against his messengers. Confident - Secure and insensible of his danger.
14 Foolishly - His passion hurries him into foolish speeches and actions. Wicked devices - One who suppresses his passion, but meditates revenge. Hated - Both by God and men.
15 Inherit - They possess it as their inheritance, holding it fast, and glorying in it. Knowledge - The saving knowledge of God and of their own duty.
16 The evil - Good men shall have the pre - eminency over the wicked.
17 Hated - Despised and abandoned.
18 Despiseth - That does not pity and relieve the poor.
20 The talk - Idle talking will bring a man to poverty.
21 Riches - They are a singular advantage and ornament to them. But - As for rich fools, their folly is not cured, but made worse and more manifest by their riches.
22 Souls - Such as are innocent, from false accusations.
