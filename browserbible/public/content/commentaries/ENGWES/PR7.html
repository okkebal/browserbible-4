1 Parteth - Gives to every one what is right or meet.
3 Satisfied - Wise discourses tend to the satisfaction of the speaker, as well as to the good of the hearers.
4 Death and life - Are brought upon men by, the good or bad use of their tongues. Lovest - That love much talking.
5 A wise - A good wife; one that deserves the name, and performs the duty of that relation. Favour - Obtains her not by his own diligence, but by God's good providence.
7 A man - Heb. A man of friends; who desires the friendship of others.
9 A man - Heb. A man of friends; who desires the friendship of others.
10 Hasteth - That rashly and headily rushes into actions.
11 Perverteth - Blasts his designs and enterprises. Fretteth - He ascribes his unhappiness not to his own sin, but to God and his providence.
14 A friend - in profession.
15 Brethren - His nearest relations. Hate - Despise and shun him. Pursueth - Earnestly imploring their pity.
18 Delight - To live in pleasure and outward glory, doth not become him, nor suit with him; because prosperity corrupts even wise men, and makes fools mad; and because it gives him more opportunity to discover his folly, and to do mischief both to himself and others.
21 Dropping - Are like rain continually dropping upon an house.
23 Casteth - Makes a man careless, and like one asleep in his business.
24 His ways - The commands of God.
26 While - Before custom in sin, and thy indulgence have made him incorrigible. Let not - Forbear not to give him due and necessary correction.
27 Great wrath - Of strong passions. Deliver - If thou forbear to punish him. Do it - Thou must deliver him again and again.
