3 But - God will declare himself to be a righteous judge, and will again establish justice in the earth. Follow - They will all approve of it, and imitate this justice of God in all their actions.
4 Rise - To defend and help me. I looked hither and thither, but none appeared; God alone helped me.
5 Dwelt - In the place of silence, the grave.
7 Thoughts - While my heart was filled with various and perplexing thoughts, as this Hebrew word signifies. Comforts - Thy promises, and the remembrance of my former experience of thy care and kindness.
8 Shall - Wilt thou take part with the unrighteous powers of the world who oppress thy people. A law - By virtue of those unrighteous decrees which they have made.
15 God's - Above all that are called God's angels, earthly potentates, and especially the false gods of the Heathen.
16 Hand - Under his government. Strength - The strongest or highest mountains.
19 Pasture - Whom he feeds and keeps in his own pasture, or in the land which he hath appropriated to himself. The sheep - Which are under his special care. Today - Forthwith or presently.
20 Harden not - By obstinate unbelief. Provocation - In that bold and wicked contest with God in the wilderness. Temptation - In the day in which you tempted me.
21 Works - Both of mercy, and of justice.
22 Do err - Their hearts are insincere and bent to backsliding. Not known - After all my teaching and discoveries of myself to them; they did not know, nor consider, those great things which I had wrought for them.
23 My rest - Into the promised land, which is called the rest, (Deut 12:9).
25 O sing - Upon this new and great occasion, not the removal of the ark, but the coming of the Messiah.
28 Gods - The gods of the nations, as the next verse expounds it.
30 Before him - In his presence.
33 Beauty - Cloathed with all the gifts and graces, which are necessary in God's worship.
34 Reigneth - God hath now set up his kingdom in the world. Established - The nations of the world shall by the means of it enjoy an established and lasting peace.
37 Before - At the presence and approach of their Lord and Maker. Cometh - To set up his throne among all the nations of the earth.
39 Isles - The Gentile nations, as this word, used (Isa 42:4), is expounded, (Matt 12:21).
40 Darkness - A dark cloud doth encompass him.
44 Heavens - The angels, yea God himself from heaven.
45 Confounded - Let them be ashamed of their folly. Gods - All you whom the Gentiles have made the objects of their worship.
46 Zion - Thy people dwelling in Zion or Jerusalem, to whom Christ came. Heard - The fame of thy judgments, and the setting up the kingdom of the Messiah.
49 Light - Joy and happiness. Sown - Is laid up for them.
53 Salvation - The redemption of the world by the Messiah: which was hitherto reserved was a secret among the Jews, yea was not throughly known by the most of the Jews themselves. Righteousness - His faithfulness in accomplishing this great promise.
62 People - Such as are enemies to God and his people. Sitteth - Upon the ark. He is present with his people. Earth - The people of the earth. Moved - With fear and trembling.
64 Them - All people.
65 Judgment - Though his dominion be absolute, and his power irresistible, yet he manages it with righteousness. The king's strength is by a known Hebraism put for the strong, or powerful king. Equity - In all thy proceedings.
66 Foot - stool - Before the ark, which is so called, (1Chron 28:2). Holy - It is consecrated to be a pledge of God's presence.
67 Moses - Moses before the institution of the priesthood executed that office, (Exod 24:6). That call - Who used frequently and solemnly to intercede with God on the behalf of the people.
68 Spake - To some of them: to Moses and Aaron, (Exod 19:24 33:9 )- 11 1Sam 7:9, &c.
