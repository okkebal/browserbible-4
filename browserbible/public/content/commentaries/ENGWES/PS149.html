1 Are not - Both they and their families suddenly perish.
3 Despised - That lives in a mean condition. Honoureth - That glories in his high birth or gay attire.
4 Regardeth - He will not destroy it either by labour beyond its strength, or by denying it necessary food or rest. Cruel - There is cruelty mixed even with their most merciful actions.
5 Tilleth - That employs his time in an honest calling. Vain persons - In an idle course of living.
6 Desireth - He approves those arts, which wicked men use like nets to ensnare other men. The root - That piety, which is the root of his actions, yields him sufficient fruit both for his own need, and to do good to others.
8 Mouth - By his pious and profitable discourses. Hands - Of his actions.
9 Hearkeneth - That distrusts his own judgments, and seeks counsel from others.
