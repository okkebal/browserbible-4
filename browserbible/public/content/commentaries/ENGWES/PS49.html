8 Gall - Instead of giving me that comfort which my condition required, they added to my afflictions. Vinegar - These things were metaphorically fulfilled in David, but properly in Christ, the description of whose sufferings was principally intended here by the Holy Ghost.
9 Their table - And this punishment in their table, exactly answers their sin, in giving Christ gall for his meat, ver.(21). A snare - Their table or meat, which is set before them, shall become a snare: the occasion of their destruction.
10 Eyes - Not the eyes of their bodies, but of their minds: as they that shut their eyes and will not see, so they shall be judicially blinded. To shake - To take away their strength.
13 For - Which is an act of barbarous cruelty. Talk - Reproaching them, and triumphing in their calamities.
14 Wilt add - Give them up to their own lusts. Not let them - Partake of thy righteousness, or of thy mercy and goodness.
15 Living - Of eternal life.
16 On high - Out of the reach of mine enemies.
18 This - This hearty sacrifice of praise, is more grateful to God, than the most glorious legal sacrifices. Hath horns - That is both tender and mature, as it is when the horns bud forth, and the hoofs grow hard.
19 The humble - Those pious persons who are grieved for their calamities, will heartily rejoice in my deliverance. Live - Or, be revived, which were dejected, and in a manner dead with sorrow.
20 Prisoners - Those who are in prison or affliction for his sake.
