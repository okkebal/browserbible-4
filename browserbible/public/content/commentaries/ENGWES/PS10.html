1 Profit - What wilt thou gain by it? The dust - Shall they that are dead celebrate thy goodness in the land of the living? Or, shall my dust praise thee?
3 Sackcloth - Given me occasion to put off that sackcloth, which they used to wear in times of mourning, (Esth 4:1 Psal 35:13 Isa 32:11 Joel 1:13). Girded - With joy, as with a garment, surrounding me on every side.
4 My glory - My tongue.
6 Ashamed - Of my confidence in thy promise. Deliver me - According to thy faithfulness and goodness.
10 My spirit - My soul or life; to preserve it from the malice of mine enemies. For - Thou hast delivered me formerly, and therefore I commit myself to thee for the future. O Lord, &c. - Who hast shewed thyself so, in making good thy promise.
11 Vanities - Idols, which are often called Vanities, as (Deut 32:21). Or, curious arts, and all sorts of divinations.
12 Known - Loved me, and cared for me.
13 Room - Made way for me to escape, when I was encompassed by them.
14 Grief - With continual weeping.
15 Iniquity - For the punishment of mine iniquity. Consumed - The juice and marrow of them bring almost dried up with grief.
16 A fear - They were afraid to give me any countenance or assistance. Fled - To prevent their own danger and ruin.
17 A broken vessel - Which is irreparable, and useless, and therefore despised by all.
18 Fear - Just cause of fear.
