2 Tumult - No less wild and impetuous.
3 Thy tokens - Terrible thunders and lightnings, and earthquakes, and comets or other strange meteors, or works of God in the air. Morning - The successive courses of the morning and evening; or of the sun and moon which go forth at those times. Thus the whole verse speaks of the natural works of God, the former clause, of such as are extraordinary and terrible, the latter of such as are ordinary and delightful.
4 River - With rain, which he very significantly calls a river for its plenty, and the river of God, of God's immediate providing. Them - The inhabitants of the earth. Provided - Or, disposed, the earth, which without this would be hard and barren.
5 Bringest down - For the rain dissolves the high and hard clods of earth.
7 Wilderness - Which though neglected by men, are furnished with food for beasts.
8 Sing - They are abundantly satisfied with thy goodness, and in their manner sing forth the praise of their benefactor.
10 All lands - Ye people of all nations. He invites the Gentile world, to the contemplation and celebration of God's works.
15 We - Our nation, or our ancestors, in whose loins we then were.
19 Proved us - As it were in a burning furnace; and with a design to purge out our dross.
20 Net - Which our enemies laid for us.
21 To ride - To use us like slaves.
24 I will go - One speaks in the name of all the rest. Incense - With the fat of rams, which is no less pleasing to God than incense.
27 Iniquity - Any sin. In heart - If my heart had been false to God, although I might have forborne outward acts. If I had been guilty of that, by heart was set upon sin, or I desired only that which I resolved in my heart to spend upon my lusts.
