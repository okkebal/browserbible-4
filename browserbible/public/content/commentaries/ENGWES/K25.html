2 He died - The history is briefly and imperfectly described here, and the defects supplied in (the book of Chronicles, is great part written for that end, to supply things omitted in the book of Kings) out of both it may be thus compleated: he fled first to Megiddo, and thence to Samaria, where he was caught, and thence brought to Jehu, and by his sentence was put to death at Megiddo.
6 Had Zimri - Remember thy brother traitor Zimri had but a very short enjoyment of the benefit of his treason.
9 And said - It seems he had forgot the charge given him above, ver.(10). A king's daughter - He doth not say, because she was a king's wife, lest he should seem to shew any respect to that wicked house of Ahab, which God had devoted to utter destruction.
18 The house - The chief governor of the kings palace. City - The chief magistrate or military governor.
20 Sent them - Jehu justly required this, because the sovereign lord of all mens lives commanded it, but the Samaritans wickedly obeyed it, without any knowledge of, or regard to God's command.
24 Left none - In that place and kingdom; for he did leave some of the royal seed of Judah.
