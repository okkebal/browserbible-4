1 My terrors - As my people were wont to be called together from all parts in a solemn day, so now my terrible enemies, or terrible things are by thee called together.
3 I am the man - It seems, this is spoken in the name of the people, who were before set out under the notion of a woman.
6 Made old - All my beauty is gone, and all my strength.
7 Builded - He hath built forts and batteries against my walls and houses.
11 Enclosed - He has defeated all my methods and counsels for security, by insuperable difficulties like walls of hewn stone. Crooked - Nay, God not only defeated their counsels, but made them fatal and pernicious to them.
17 Wormwood - With severe and bitter dispensations.
18 Ashes - Mourners were wont to throw ashes on their heads.
21 Wormwood - Wormwood and gall, are often made use of to signify great affliction.
23 This - Which follows, concerning the nature of God, and his good providences.
25 Faithfulness - In fulfilling thy promises to thy people.
29 Bear - Quietly and patiently to bear what afflictions God will please to lay upon us. And if God tame us when young, by his word or by his rod, it is an unspeakable advantage.
30 Borne it - That he keep his soul in subjection to God, because God hath humbled him by his rod.
