1 Also - They neither love, nor hate, nor envy any thing in this world, but are unconcerned in what is done under the sun.
3 White - The eastern people of the best sort, used white garments, especially in times of rejoicing. Ointment - Which upon joyful occasions was poured upon mens heads.
4 Vanity - Of this vain and frail life.
5 Whatsoever - Whatever thou hast opportunity and ability to do, do it with unwearied diligence, and vigour and expedition. For - Thou canst neither design nor act any thing there tending to thy own comfort or advantage.
6 But time - There are times or seasons, casual to men, but known by God, in which alone he will give men success.
7 His time - The time of his death, or other distress which God is bringing upon him. Are taken - While they are sporting and feeding themselves. When - When they are most careless and secure.
8 This wisdom - I have observed this among many other instances of wisdom. Which he adds for the commendation of wisdom, notwithstanding its insufficiency for man's happiness without God's blessing.
10 Yet - He was soon neglected and his great service forgotten.
12 Of wise men - Though poor. In quiet - Uttered with a modest and low voice. The cry - The clamorous discourses of a rich and potent, but foolish man.
16 Heart - His understanding is always present with him and ready to direct him. He mentions the right hand, because that is the common instrument of action. A fool's - His understanding is not effectual to govern his affections and actions.
17 Walketh - In his daily conversation. He saith - He discovers his folly to all that meet him.
18 The spirit - The passion. Leave not - In anger or discontent. Continue in a diligent and faithful discharge of thy duty, and modestly and humbly submit to him. Yielding - A gentle and submissive carriage.
20 The rich - Wise and worthy men, rich in endowments of mind.
22 An hedge - Whereby another man's fields or vineyards are distinguished, that he may either take away their fruits, or enlarge his own fields.
23 Whoso removeth - Stones too heavy for them: who rashly attempts things too high and hard for them.
24 Wisdom - As wisdom instructs a man in the smallest matters, so it is useful for a man's direction in all weighty affairs.
25 Without - If not prevented by the art and care of the charmer; which practice he does not justify, but only mentions by way of resemblance.
26 Gracious - Procure him favour with those who hear him.
28 Full of words - Forward to promise and boast what he will do, whereas none can be sure of future events, even during his own life, much more after his death.
