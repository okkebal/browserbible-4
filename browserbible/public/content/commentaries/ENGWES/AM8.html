1 For - It is time to seek God; for your neighbours, as well as you, shall be destroyed. Gaza - A chief city of the Philistines. They - The Babylonians. Shall drive - Into captivity. At the noon day - It shall be taken by force at noon.
2 The inhabitants - All the Philistines. Cherethites - Or destroyers, men that were stout, fierce, and terrible to their neighbours. O Canaan - That part that the Philistines kept by force from the Jews.
3 For shepherds - Instead of cities full of rich citizens, there shall be only cottages for shepherds.
4 The coast - The sea - coast, the land of the Philistines. The remnant - That survive the captivity. Shall feed - Their flocks. In the houses - In places where these formerly stood. They - Both shepherds and flocks. Shall visit - In mercy.
6 Of nettles - Not cultivated, but over - run with nettles. Salt - pits - A dry, barren earth, fit only to dig salt out of. The residue - That return out of Babylon. Possess them - Settle upon those parts of their lands, that are fit for habitation.
8 Famish - Take away all their sacrifices and drink - offerings. The gods - Idols of those lands. From his place - Not only at Jerusalem, but every where.
9 By my sword - The Chaldeans are called God's sword; because God employed them.
10 He - God. The north - Assyria, which lay northward of Judea, and due north from Babylon.
11 All the beasts - All sorts of beasts which are found in those countries. The bittern - A bird that delights in desolate places.
12 This - So the prophet triumphs over her. There is none - None like me, or that can contend with me.
14 To her - Jerusalem.
