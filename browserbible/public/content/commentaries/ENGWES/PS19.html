1 Rest - Do not repine at his dealings, but quietly submit to his will, and wait for his help.
2 Fret not - Either against the sinner for his success; or against God. Do evil - If grief arise in thee, take care that it do not transport thee to sin.
3 The earth - This for the most part was literally fulfilled in that state of the church.
4 Not be - He shall be dead and gone. Diligently - Industriously seeking him. His place - His place and estate, and glory.
5 But - Those who patiently bear God's afflicting hand, and meekly pass by injuries. Peace - Partly of outward peace and prosperity, which God in his due time will give them: but principally of inward peace, in the sense of God's favour and the assurance of endless happiness.
7 His day - The day appointed by God for his punishment or destruction.
12 Knoweth - Observes with care and affection. The days - All things which befal them, their dangers and fears, and suffering. For ever - To them and their seed for ever: and when they die their inheritance is not lost, but exchanged for one infinitely better.
14 Fat - Which in an instant melts before the fire.
