1 Rebellion - Against God. Messenger - Or, a cruel angel, the angel of death, the devil, or some bloody men employed by God to avenge his quarrel.
2 Folly - In the heat of his lust or passion.
4 Letteth out - By cutting the bank of a river. Leave off - Avoid the occasions, and prevent the beginning of contention.
6 A price - Opportunities and abilities of getting it. No heart - Neither discretion to discern the worth of wisdom, nor any sincere desire to get it.
7 Was born - Was sent into the world for this, that he might comfort and relieve his brother in adversity.
8 Friend - Debtor, for whom being his friend, he becomes surety.
10 No good - Shall not get that advantage which he expects.
12 Doth good - Even to the body; it contributes much to bodily health and vigour. Drieth - Wastes the marrow of the bones, and the moisture and strength of the body.
14 In the face - His wisdom appears even in his looks. The eyes - Minding most, those things which are remotest from him, and least concern him.
16 To strike - Nor to smite magistrates, either with the hand or tongue, for the execution of justice.
20 Desire - Thro' desire of wisdom, a man having separated himself from the company, and noise, and business of the world, seeketh and intermeddleth with all wisdom, uses all diligence, that he may search and find out all solid knowledge and true wisdom.
21 Understanding - In getting sound and saving, knowledge. The discovery - In uttering that folly and wickedness which is in his heart.
22 Cometh - Into any place or company. Contempt - He despiseth all instruction and reproof. Reproach - And he shews his contempt by ignominious and reproachful expressions.
23 A man's - A wise man's. As a brook - That wisdom which is in his heart is continually pouring forth wise and good counsels.
27 They go - They wound mortally.
28 Is brother - Though they take several paths, yet both come to one end.
29 The name - The Lord, as he hath revealed himself in his works, and in his word by his promises.
33 Sustain - Will easily support him under any outward troubles. Wounded - Dejected with the sense of its own guilt and misery.
