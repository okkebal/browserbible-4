1 To depart - To preserve men from destructive courses.
3 Exalteth - Heb. lifteth up, like a banner, makes it known and visible to all men.
4 A sound heart - Free from envy and inordinate passions. Is life - Procures and maintains the health and vigour of the body.
5 His maker - Whose image the poor man bears, by whose providence he is made poor, and who hath declared himself to be their protector and avenger.
6 Driven away - In his death, from God's favour and presence. Death - In his greatest dangers and distresses, yea even in death itself.
7 Resteth - Is laid up and hid there. In the midst - In the heart. Made known - They will publish in all times and companies.
9 Shame - Both to himself, by his foolish management of the king's affairs, and to the king who made so foolish a choice of a servant.
11 Shame - Both to himself, by his foolish management of the king's affairs, and to the king who made so foolish a choice of a servant.
12 Aright - Expressing what he knows prudently and gracefully. Poureth out - Plentifully, continually, and vehemently, as a fountain does waters.
14 Wholesome - Which utters sound and useful counsels. Tree of life - Is greatly useful to preserve the present life, and to promote the spiritual and eternal life, both of the speaker and hearers. Perverseness - False or corrupt speeches. A breach - Disturbs and wounds the spirits, both of the speaker and hearers.
16 Revenues - Though he may obtain great revenues, yet they are attended with much trouble.
17 Disperse - Freely communicate to others what they know.
18 Sacrifice - All the religious services, yea, the most costly; one kind being put for all the rest. Prayer - The cheapest and meanest services.
21 Destruction - The place of destruction, of which men know nothing but by Divine revelation.
24 Foolishness - Wickedness is meat and drink to them.
25 Afflicted - Of the troubled in mind. Evil - Tedious and uncomfortable. Chearful - Heb. of a good heart.
26 Trouble - Tumultuous lusts and passions, vexatious cares, and horrors of conscience.
29 An hedge - As a way hedged up with thorns, troublesome, perplexed, and full of difficulties. Plain - Is easy and pleasant to him, notwithstanding all its difficulties.
32 Counsel - When men do not seek, or will not receive advice from others.
33 The answer - By a wise, or good and seasonable answer.
34 The way - The way a wise man takes to obtain life, is to place his heart, and treasure, and conversation on things above.
35 The proud - Of the mighty oppressors. The border - The estate, the border being often used, for the land within the borders.
