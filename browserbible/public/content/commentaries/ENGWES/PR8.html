2 The desire - The hearty desire of being kind is accepted by God, and should be owned by men as a real kindness. A poor man - Who is not able to give what he desires to do. A liar - Than a rich man who feeds the poor with fair promises, but doth not perform what he pretends.
3 Satisfied - Shall be fully contented with God's favour and blessing.
4 Bring it - To feed himself; he expects that the meat should drop into his mouth.
5 Scorner - An obstinate and impudent transgressor. Simple - Who sin thro' ignorance, being possibly drawn to it by the scorner's evil counsel or example. Reprove - A verbal reproof will be more effectual for his reformation, than the severest punishments will be to a scorner.
6 Wasteth - His father's estate. Chaseth - Causes her to avoid his presence, and to go from the house where he is.
8 Scorneth - Hath no reverence to the place of justice, nor to the presence of God there. Devoureth - Utters it with greediness and delight.
9 Prepared - Although they be deferred for a time, yet they are treasured up for them.
11 A mocker - Wine immoderately drunk makes men mockers. Raging - Makes men full of rage.
12 The fear - The terror which the wrath of a king causes.
13 Meddling - Is always ready to begin strife, and obstinate in the continuance of it.
15 Counsel - Designs of doing something of moment. Deep water - Is secret and hard to be discovered.
18 His eyes - With his very looks, or by his diligent inspection into affairs.
20 Divers - One greater for shew and one lesser for use.
21 Is known - The future disposition of a man may be probably conjectured from his childish manners.
23 Open - Shake off sloth and betake thyself to thy employment with diligence and vigour.
25 The lips - But wise speeches are of far greater worth.
26 Take - As a pledge, without which he ought not to be trusted. Of him - That is surety.
28 Established - The way to bring our purposes to good effect is to manage them with serious consideration.
30 Lamp - His name and memory shall utterly perish.
34 His way - What the issue of his designs will be.
35 A snare - It brings guilt upon him. After - After a man has made vows to enquire for ways to break them.
36 The wheel - As the cart - wheel was anciently turned over the sheaves to beat the corn out of them. He punishes them as their offences deserve.
