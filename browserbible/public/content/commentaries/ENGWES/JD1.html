1 I found of thy children - Probably in their aunt's house, (2Jo 1:13). Walking in the truth - In faith and love.
2 That which we had from the beginning - Of our Lord's ministry. Indeed it was, in some sense, from the beginning of the world. That we may love one another - More abundantly.
3 And this is the proof of true love, universal obedience built on the love of God. This - Love. Is the great commandment which ye have heard from the beginning - Of our preaching.
4 Carefully keep what ye have heard from the beginning, for many seducers are entered into the world, who confess not Jesus Christ that came in the flesh - Who disbelieve either his prophetic, or priestly, or kingly office. Whosoever does this is the seducer - From God. And the antichrist - Fighting against Christ.
5 That we lose not the things which we have wrought - Which every apostate does. But receive a full reward - Having fully employed all our talents to the glory of him that gave them. Here again the apostle modestly transfers it to himself.
6 Receive this as a certain rule: Whosoever transgresseth - Any law of God. Hath not God - For his Father and his God. He that abideth in the doctrine of Christ - Believing and obeying it. He hath both the Father and the Son - For his God.
7 If any came to you - Either as a teacher or a brother. And bring not this doctrine - That is, advance anything contrary to it. Receive him not into your house - As either a teacher or a brother - Neither bid him God speed - Give him no encouragement therein.
8 For he that biddeth him God speed - That gives him any encouragement, is accessory to his evil deeds.
9 Having many things to write, I was not minded to write now - Only of these, which were then peculiarly needful.
10 The children of thy elect or Christian sister - Absent, if not dead, when the apostle wrote this.
12 Keep yourselves from idols - From all worship of false gods, from all worship of images or of any creature, and from every inward idol; from loving, desiring, fearing anything more than God. Seek all help and defence from evil, all happiness in the true God alone.
13 Caius was probably that Caius of Corinth whom St. Paul mentions, (Rom 16:23). If so, either he was removed from Achaia into Asia, or St. John sent this letter to Corinth.
15 For - I know thou usest all thy talents to his glory. The truth that is in thee - The true faith and love.
16 I have no greater joy than this - Such is the spirit of every true Christian pastor. To hear that my children walk in the truth - Caius probably was converted by St. Paul. Therefore when St. John speaks of him. with other believers, as his children, it may be considered as the tender style of paternal love, whoever were the instruments of their conversion. And his using this appellation, when writing under the character of the elder, has its peculiar beauty.
17 Faithfully - Uprightly and sincerely.
18 Who have testified of thy love before the church - The congregation with whom I now reside. Whom if thou send forward on their journey - Supplied with what is needful. Thou shalt do well - How tenderly does the apostle enjoin this!
19 They went forth - To preach the gospel.
20 To receive - With all kindness. The truth - Which they preach.
21 I wrote to the church - Probably that to which they came. But Diotrephes - Perhaps the pastor of it. Who loveth to have the preeminence among them - To govern all things according to his own will. Receiveth us not - Neither them nor me. So did the mystery of iniquity already work!
22 He prateth against us - Both them and me, thereby endeavouring to excuse himself.
23 Follow not that which is evil - In Diotrephes. But that which is good - In Demetrius. He hath not seen God - Is a stranger to him.
24 And from the truth itself - That is, what they testify is the very truth. Yea, we also bear testimony - I and they that are with me.
