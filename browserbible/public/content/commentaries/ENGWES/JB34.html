1 Morning - Every morning. As soon as I wake, I am still with thee, as he saith, (Psal 139:18). The first thing that I do is to pray to thee.
2 Surely - Thou dost not approve of, nor delight in them, or in their prayers. Dwelt - Have any fellowship with thee.
5 Come - With holy boldness and confidence. Mercy - Trusting only in thy great mercy. Fear - With an holy dread and reverence of thy majesty. Towards - Looking towards it, when I cannot come to it.
6 Righteousness - In thy righteous laws. Because - That I may give them no occasion of slandering me, or religion for my sake. The way - The way wherein thou wouldst have to one walk. Plain - That I may clearly discern it, and readily walk in it.
7 Throat - Wide opened ready to devour all that come within their reach. A metaphor from wild beasts gaping for the prey.
8 Destroy - Condemn and punish them. Cast - Out of thy land, and from among thy people.
13 Bones - My inmost parts.
16 In death - Among the dead. Remembrance - He speaks of the remembrance or celebration of God's grace in the land of the living, to the edification of God's church, and the propagation of true religion among men; which is not done in the other life.
17 With my tears - It well becomes the greatest spirits to be tender, and to relent under the tokens of God's displeasure. David who could face Goliath himself, melts into tears at the remembrance of sin, and under the apprehension of Divine wrath, and it is no diminution to his character.
19 Hath heard - By the workings of God's grace upon his heart, he knew his prayer was accepted. His tears had a voice, in the ears of the God of mercy. Silent tears are no speechless ones. Our tears are cries to God.
21 Ashamed - Of their vain confidence. Return - Repent of their sins and return to their obedience.
23 Ashamed - Of their vain confidence. Return - Repent of their sins and return to their obedience.
24 Lest - Mine enemy. Tear - Out of my body.
25 Hands - Which Cush and others falsely lay to my charge. Iniquity - In my actions.
26 Deliver - When it was in my power to destroy him, as (1Sam 24:2 )- 6.
28 Lift up - Glorify thyself, and shew thyself to be above them. Commanded - To execute that righteous sentence, which thou hast commanded, appointed, and declared by thy prophet Samuel.
29 Compass - They will come from all parts to worship thee, and offer to thee praises and sacrifices. High - To thy tribunal, to sit there and judge my cause. An allusion to earthly tribunals, which generally are set up on high above the people.
33 Every day - Even then, when his providence seems to favour them, and they are most secure and confident.
34 He will - God will hasten, and speedily execute his judgments upon him.
35 Him - For the wicked. Ordaineth - Designs or fits for this very use. Of all sinners, persecutors are set up as the fairest marks of Divine wrath. They set God at defiance but cannot set themselves out of the reach of his judgments.
36 Travelleth - This metaphor denotes his deep design, and vigorous endeavours for doing mischief, and his restlessness and pain 'till he have accomplished it.
