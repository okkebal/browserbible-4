1 A good word - A compassionate or encouraging word.
2 Neighbour - Than any other men. Seduceth - Heb. maketh them to err, to lose that excellency or happiness which they had promised themselves.
3 Resteth not - Does not enjoy the fruit of his labours. Precious - Yields him comfort and blessing with it.
7 Shall eat - Shall receive comfort, and benefit by his wise and profitable discourses.
11 Keepeth him - From that over - throw which befal sinners.
12 Maketh rich - Some men who have little or nothing, pretend to have great riches. Maketh rich - Some rich men profess to be poor.
13 Riches - Riches enable a man to redeem his life when it is in greatest danger, and poverty preserves a man from many injuries. Because they are cautious, that they may not provoke others; and because they are esteemed objects of pity.
14 The light - The prosperity. Rejoiceth - Shines with a pleasant and constant brightness. Rejoicing is here ascribed to the light, as it is to the sun, (Psal 19:5), metaphorically. Put out - Their felicity shall have a hidden end.
15 Pride - It is chiefly, pride which blows up the coals of contention. Well - advised - Who are not governed by their passions, but by prudent considerations. Wisdom - Which teaches them to avoid contention.
16 Vanity - By wicked practices.
17 Hope deferred - Delays in obtaining what a man passionately desires. The desire - The good desired. A tree of life - It is satisfactory, and reviving.
18 The word - The word of God.
19 The law - The instruction, or counsel; as the word law, is frequently understood in scripture.
20 Giveth - Makes a man acceptable to God. Rough - Offensive and hateful to God and men, as rough ways are to a traveller.
21 Knowledge - Considerately and discreetly. Layeth open - By his foolish actions.
22 A messenger - Who is unfaithful in the execution of that which is committed to his charge. Is health - Procures benefit, as to his master, so to himself.
24 Sweet - Whatsoever men earnestly desire, the enjoyment of it is sweet to them; therefore sinners rejoice in the satisfaction of their sinful lusts, and abhor all restraint of them.
26 Evil - Punishment proportionable to their sin.
28 The poor - Poor persons by their diligent labours, and God's blessing often grow rich. Destroyed - Or, consumed, brought to poverty, for want of discretion.
29 Hateth his son - His fond affection, is as pernicious to him as hatred.
32 Buildeth - Maintains and improves her family and estate. Her hands - By her idleness or sin.
