1 My knowledge - The knowledge of God, which I am here delivering.
2 Fitted - Fitly expressed: thou wilt be able to discourse profitably of them.
4 In counsels and knowledge - In counsels which proceed from found knowledge.
5 Answer - That thou mayst be able to give solid, and satisfactory answers.
6 In the gate - Under pretence of justice.
7 Spoil the soul - Take away not only their goods but their lives too.
13 Before kings - He is fit to be employed in the affairs of princes.
15 Ruler - With a great man.
16 Put a knife - Restrain thine appetite, as if a man stood with a knife at thy throat.
17 Deceitful - They do not yield thee that satisfaction which thou didst expect from them.
18 Thine own wisdom - From worldly wisdom, which persuades men to use all possible means to get riches.
19 Set thine eyes - Look upon it with earnestness and desire. Is not - Which has no solid and settled being. Eagle - Swiftly, strongly, and irrecoverably.
20 Evil eye - Of the envious or covetous man.
21 So is he - You are not to judge of him by his words, but by the constant temper of his mind.
22 Vomit up - When thou perceivest his churlish disposition, his meat will be loathsome to thee. Sweet words - Thy pleasant discourse wherewith thou didst design to delight and profit him.
23 Of a fool - Cast not away good counsels upon incorrigible sinners.
24 Enter not - To possess their lands.
25 Redeemer - Heb. their near kinsman, to whom it belongs to avenge their wrongs, and to recover and maintain their rights.
27 Shall not die - It is a likely way to prevent his destruction.
30 My reins - I shall rejoice with all my soul.
