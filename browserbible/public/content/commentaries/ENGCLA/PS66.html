For want of astronomical knowledge, the poor Jews were put to sad shifts to know the real time of the new moon. They generally
18 sent persons to the top of some hill or mountain about the time which, according to their supputations, the new moon should appear. The first who saw it was to give immediate notice to the Sanhedrin; they closely examined the reporter as to his credibility, and whether his information agreed with their calculations.
